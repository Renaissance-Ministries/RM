The Alignment and the Watchman: Amir Tsarfati's June 30 Update, the Ezekiel Horizon, and the Sons of Issachar Standard

By: Thomas Lee Abshier, ND

Date: July 3, 2026

Fellowship Discussion Essay | 3 July 2026, revised 7 July 2026

Occasion. On June 30, 2026, Amir Tsarfati released one of his periodic video updates through his ministry, Behold Israel (https://beholdisrael.org). Tsarfati is an Israeli believer, a former IDF officer, and one of the most widely followed Bible-prophecy teachers in the world; his updates are valuable to us because he is an informed Israeli reading Israeli sources from inside his nation's war, and because he asks what the events mean against the map the Hebrew prophets drew twenty-six centuries ago. This update covers the American memorandum of understanding with Iran, a new Israel–Lebanon agreement brokered by Secretary Rubio, the demolition of Hezbollah's underground fortifications, Iran's leverage over the Strait of Hormuz, and Turkey's hardening posture — and Tsarfati closes by locating all of it on the Ezekiel 38 horizon, calling the moment "a step closer to the Ezekiel war."

But this essay is not, finally, about how to grade his report. It is about a standard the fellowship carries on its own banner without noticing. Our tagline is 1 Chronicles 12:38 — of one heart to make Christ King. Six verses above it, in the same muster roll of the tribes gathering to David, stands the tribe this essay is named for: the children of Issachar, which were men that had understanding of the times, to know what Israel ought to do (1 Chronicles 12:32). Mark the grammar: understanding of the times is not the deliverable. It is the input. The deliverable is what ought to be done. An essay that ends at understanding — that weighs the watchman's report, sorts the evidence into tiers, and adjourns — has stopped six verses short of its own banner. This essay does not adjourn there. It moves in three parts, and the parts must come in order: first the report and the epistemics (briefly, because hygiene is a paragraph, not a life's work); then the text of Ezekiel and where our eschatology places it; then the deliberation and the action — the Issachar sections, which are the point.

I. What Amir Reports

His account, fairly and in his framing. The United States negotiated a memorandum of understanding with Iran — talks at Lake Lucerne, signature at Versailles. His judgment is scathing: worse than Munich, he argues, because Chamberlain appeased before a world war revealed what Hitler was, whereas this MOU was signed after a forty-day war in which Iran was beaten to the floor — and it revives the IRGC with oxygen and billions, and treats Iran as the guardian of Lebanon, obligating Israeli withdrawal and throwing Hezbollah a lifeline. Against it he sets the Rubio-brokered fourteen-point agreement between Israel and the elected Lebanese government — the Lebanese, he reports, having concluded they fear Iranian guardianship more than Israeli presence — permitting the IDF to remain in southern Lebanon until every militia outside the Lebanese Armed Forces is dismantled. He shows the demolition of the enormous Hezbollah fortress under Majdal Zoun and reports the destruction of a ten-mile Gaza tunnel system neutralized with thirty thousand cubic meters of concrete. He describes Iran's remaining weapon — the Strait of Hormuz, attacked repeatedly with token American response — and Iran's open refusal to meet. He plays the President's unguarded remark that Erdoğan "stayed out of the war," which Amir reads as an admission that Turkey nearly entered it on Iran's side; he reports — citing an Israeli general, a ministerial hint, and an Emirati official — the claim that a Mossad plan to arm the Kurds and Iranian minorities against the regime was leaked by the Vice President to Erdoğan, who smothered it; he reports Erdoğan's declaration that Zionism has set its sights on Turkey, Netanyahu's cabinet reply that Israel takes annihilation rhetoric seriously, Israel's formal recognition of the Armenian genocide, and the administration's intention to sell Turkey F-35s. He reports a statistician's analysis finding the Hamas-ministry casualty figures internally incoherent, and famine photography that concealed medical context. And he closes on the horizon: Iran emboldened, Turkey positioning, Russia estranged, Libya and Sudan in the wings — the Ezekiel coalition assembling.

II. The Watchman's Epistemics: Assertion, Action, and the Adversary's Archive

Now the epistemics — compressed to its proper size, and then deepened where it actually needs depth.

The hygiene first, in one movement. Amir's report sorts into three tiers, and the fellowship should hold each at its level: public verifiable events (the agreements and their published points, the filmed demolitions, Erdoğan's speeches, the Armenian recognition, the Hormuz attacks); informed interpretation (the Munich comparison, the motive attributions, the read of Lebanese and Iranian intentions); and single-chained claims (the leak, the statistics, the famine-photo analysis) — which we may not assert as established fact, because the ninth commandment governs what we affirm, and he that is first in his own cause seemeth just; but his neighbour cometh and searcheth him (Proverbs 18:17). One caution inside our own camp proves the tier system earns its keep — and this one has a happy ending that teaches the whole method. A figure of $300 billion for the MOU circulated in the channels that carried this story to some of us, while Amir's own update said only "billions." Checked against the record, the figure verifies — with a precision that matters: Al Jazeera, quoting the MOU text directly, reports that the document commits the US to undertake with regional partners a plan of "at least $300 billion" for Iran's reconstruction and economic development, with the implementation mechanism left to a sixty-day negotiation, the President publicly denying any direct US payment, the Vice President assigning the funding to regional Arab states and outside investors, and no country yet confirming a contribution ([aljazeera.com, June 18, 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/18/mous-300bn-iran-reconstruction-fund-becomes-us-political-flashpoint)); the same MOU separately commits to the immediate lifting of sanctions on Iran's fossil fuel industry and negotiations to unfreeze billions in Iranian assets — which independently substantiates Amir's "oxygen and billions." So the loose version ("the US pledged to give Iran $300 billion") overstated, the cautious version ("billions") understated, and the verified version — a US-brokered plan with a $300 billion floor, funders and mechanism undetermined — turns out to be more damning than the rumor, because it is undeniable. Precision is the sharper weapon. That is what the sixty seconds of checking buys.

That is the hygiene. Here is the correction this revision exists to make: the triage governs assertion; it does not govern vigilance, investigation, deliberation, or action — and treating it as if it did is not righteousness but paralysis. The two-or-three-witnesses rule of Deuteronomy 19:15 is a conviction standard: it tells the court when it may punish. It was never a standard for when the watchman may cry out, when the city may investigate, or when the council may deliberate. Read it as an action threshold and you have silenced every whistleblower, every confidential informant, every lone bystander, every Rahab — you have built a world in which the wicked act on rumor while the righteous wait for adjudication, and the adjudication never comes. Scripture names this failure mode without sympathy: He that observeth the wind shall not sow; and he that regardeth the clouds shall not reap (Ecclesiastes 11:4). Different acts carry different evidentiary thresholds. A single credible witness cannot convict — but a single credible witness absolutely triggers the investigation, the protective detail, the demand for answers. And moral deliberation requires even less, because it can proceed conditionally at full strength: if the leak occurred, here is what it was and what repair requires — a sentence that violates no commandment and surrenders no force.

And there is a second depth the first draft lacked: the character of the source is itself evidence, and weighing it is commanded, not forbidden. The simple believeth every word: but the prudent man looketh well to his going (Proverbs 14:15) — one verse holding both credulity and paralysis at arm's length. Courts have always known this; impeaching a witness's credibility is half of trial practice. And the modern age handed us the definitive parable. In 1948, Whittaker Chambers — a single witness, a confessed perjurer, an ex-Communist courier, a rumpled man with no standing — accused Alger Hiss, the most immaculately credentialed man the American establishment could produce: Harvard Law, clerk to Justice Holmes, present at Yalta, secretary-general of the founding conference of the United Nations. Under a flat two-witness rule, Chambers is dismissed on the first day. Under "weigh the credentials," Hiss wins forever — and very nearly did, while the respectable institutions closed ranks around him and poured on the establishment's favorite solvent, ridicule. What actually resolved it was the third way: the disreputable witness was taken seriously enough to be tested — and the fruits appeared, the details only a participant could know, the typed documents, the microfilm in the pumpkin. Hiss was convicted of perjury in 1950. And in 1995 the archive itself surrendered: the declassified Venona decrypts of Soviet cable traffic settled for essentially all serious historians that the penetration Chambers described had been real all along — that what a generation had been trained to call a paranoid conspiracy theory had been sitting in Moscow's own communications the entire time.

The Chambers case teaches the fellowship three things at once, and all three belong in our permanent equipment. First: single witnesses can be right, and the tier system exists to weigh them, never to dismiss them — tier three means "hold loosely and test," not "discard." Second: consensus can be manufactured, and ridicule is its favorite tool precisely because ridicule substitutes for evidence — Saul Alinsky codified it as a rule because it works, and the fellowship should feel its skin prickle whenever a claim is answered with mockery instead of documents, for woe unto them that call evil good, and good evil (Isaiah 5:20) describes an industrial process, not merely a personal vice. When state-level resources are committed to deception — and Venona proved they were, and nothing in fallen human nature has retired the practice — "everyone knows" is not evidence; it is frequently the product. Third — and this is the discipline's vindication, not its refutation: what converted Chambers's testimony from contested claim into settled history was verification. The pumpkin papers and the decrypts did what indignation never could. The triage and the courage were never enemies; the triage is what gives the courage its ammunition.

One guard rail, and the doctrine is complete. "Consider the source" must attach to demonstrated track record, never to tribe — or we become the mirror image of the whispering campaigns we deplore. An outlet or institution with a documented record of manufactured narrative has earned its discount, and applying it is Bayesian prudence, not partisanship. But the measure must be one we can survive when it is turned on us: with what measure ye mete, it shall be measured to you again (Matthew 7:2). A friendly source that inflates pays the same discount as a hostile one that lies. That symmetry is not a concession to the adversary; it is the only thing that keeps our testimony worth more than his.

III. The Ezekiel Text Itself

Now the text Amir's whole update leans against, held to the same honesty. Ezekiel 38–39 describes a war that has never occurred: a northern coalition descending on a regathered Israel — Gog, the land of Magog, the chief prince of Meshech and Tubal... Persia, Ethiopia, and Libya with them... Gomer, and all his bands; the house of Togarmah of the north quarters (38:1–6) — in the latter years (38:8), against a people brought back from the sword dwelling safely, all of them dwelling without walls (38:11), drawn down by God Himself — I will turn thee back, and put hooks into thy jaws (38:4) — for God's stated purpose of self-revelation: and they shall know that I am the LORD (38:23).

The identifications, graded honestly. Persia is Iran without a single interpretive step — the name changed only in 1935; the chief engine of the current war against Israel is named in the text outright. Meshech, Tubal, Gomer, and Togarmah are, by the sober consensus of historical geography, peoples of ancient Anatolia — the Assyrian records' Mushki and Tabal, the Cimmerians, Til-garimmu in the Armenian highlands: the landmass of modern Turkey. This is worth pausing over, because the popular prophecy tradition spent seventy years hearing Moscow and Tobolsk in those names on phonetic resemblance alone, while the historically grounded reading puts Turkey — not Russia — at the coalition's mass. Rosh is the contested term: Hebrew for "head" or "chief" (so the King James: the chief prince), read as a proper noun "Rosh" only on a disputed parsing — so Russia's participation is possible and would surprise no one, but the text requires Turkey and Persia in a way it never requires Russia. Cush and Put are the upper Nile (Sudan) and North Africa west of Egypt (Libya).

Set the moment against the text. What fits: Israel regathered after near-annihilation — the precondition that made the chapter a dead letter for eighteen centuries and a live one since 1948; Persia wounded and vengeful; the coalition's Anatolian core turning, its president speaking of Israel's existence as a threat to Turkey's survival; Sudan and Libya broken and available. And a detail the fellowship should notice: the Ezekiel coalition conspicuously lacks the inner ring — no Egypt, no Jordan, no Lebanon, no Hamas, no Hezbollah — and the news of this very update is the inner ring being dismantled, the battlefield the text presupposes being cleared before our eyes. What does not fit, and must be held with equal firmness: Israel does not yet dwell safely, without walls — Israel in July 2026 is mobilized and watching every border. It is even possible that the current agreements, if they hold, will one day manufacture the false security of 38:11 — marked speculation, offered as such. So: is Amir right that we are "a step closer"? On the alignment — yes, and the honest geography makes his case stronger than the pop-prophecy version, because it puts Turkey where the text puts the weight. On nearness — of that day and that hour knoweth no man (Mark 13:32). Closer is defensible. Close is more than anyone may say.

IV. Watching to Endure, Not Watching to Leave

Our one difference with Amir, stated plainly and with love. He teaches within the pre-tribulation system: the Church removed before the trial, the aligning events a departure board. Our fellowship, after the study in Endurance, Not Escape, holds the historic eschatology of the Church — one return of Christ, public and glorious, after tribulation, the saints kept in the trial rather than from it (John 17:15). The difference does not touch the value of his watching — what I say unto you I say unto all, Watch (Mark 13:37) belongs to no rapture position, and the alignment is the same alignment on either eschatology. What it touches is watching's purpose. The pre-trib believer watches like a traveler at the departures board; the believer of the historic eschatology watches like a garrison — and a garrison's preparation is character, fellowship, provision, courage, and a faith that has made peace in advance with loss. This is the entire logic of the Christian Underground: if the nations are aligning on the Ezekiel pattern, the endurance reading does not pack a suitcase; it deepens its roots. And it prepares for something else the traveler never trains for — which brings us to the section this essay exists for.

V. The Sons of Issachar: What Ought to Be Done

Understanding of the times, to know what Israel ought to do. The watchman has reported; the tiers are assigned; the text is open. Now the council sits, and the questions Amir's update forces are moral and political questions that deserve answers — deliberated on the merits, judged provisionally but judged, because no regime was ever unseated, no policy ever corrected, and no people ever rescued by a discussion of information fidelity. Four questions. Note as we begin how little of the deliberation depends on the contested tier-three claims: the plan's existence is now openly sourced by Israeli and Emirati officials; the MOU's shape, the F-35 intention, and Erdoğan's rhetoric are on the record. The moral case proceeds almost entirely on tier-one ground.

First: now that the plan to arm the Kurds and the Iranian people is public knowledge, should it be implemented in the open? The case for is heavy: forty-seven years of the IRGC; a population that has risen again and again — 2009, 2019, 2022 — and been shot down in the streets for lack of means; the principle that a people's right to reclaim their own government is not a favor foreign powers may withhold indefinitely; and the fact that secrecy's value is already spent — exposure converted covert support into a test of open resolve. The case for caution is not cowardice, and it has a name written in blood: Iraq, 1991, when an American administration openly called on the Iraqi people to rise, and then stood aside while Saddam's helicopters slaughtered the Shia of the south and the Kurds of the north by the tens of thousands. Half-hearted open support is the most lethal policy on the menu — it identifies the brave for the regime's gunmen and then abandons them. So the deliberation does not resolve to "arm them: yes or no." It resolves to this: either commit to support that is sustained, adequate, and survivable for the people who answer — or do not summon them to die. The middle posture, encouragement without commitment, is the one option righteousness rules out — and it is, on the visible evidence, the posture now on offer. The judgment: the plan's exposure obligates the administration to choose publicly between real commitment and honest disavowal, and citizens — including this fellowship — should demand that choice be made in daylight.

Second: should Erdoğan be opposed, and how — beginning with the F-35s? Here the record needs no tier-three assistance. A NATO head of state declares, on the record and repeatedly, rhetoric that Israel's prime minister publicly characterizes as calls for annihilation; he frames Israel's existence as a threat to Turkey's survival; and the administration proposes to sell him the alliance's premier strike fighter. The deliberation is brief because the principle is old: I will bless them that bless thee, and curse him that curseth thee (Genesis 12:3) is not a mystical abstraction — it is, at minimum, a policy instruction to nations that claim biblical inheritance, and prudence arrives at the same verdict by its own road, for weapons sold to declared eliminationism do not change the declaration; they arm it. Netanyahu's own stated standard — when someone says he intends to annihilate us, we take him seriously — is the correct one, and it binds allies too. Israel showed what moral clarity at diplomatic cost looks like in this very news cycle: recognizing the Armenian genocide, naming a historical truth Ankara has spent a century suppressing, and absorbing the consequences. The judgment: the F-35 sale should be opposed openly — by Congress, which has the statutory power to block it, and by citizens who can demand they use it — and Erdoğan's rhetoric should be treated as policy until his policy refutes it.

Third: the leak. Here the deliberation proceeds conditionally, as Section II licensed, and loses nothing by it. If an American official disclosed an allied covert plan to the leader it targeted, then what occurred was the betrayal of a people to their enemies — and not just any people, but the Kurds, whose twentieth- and twenty-first-century history is a ledger of American encouragements followed by American abandonments: 1975, when support was severed mid-revolt at the stroke of a realpolitik pen; 1991, the uprising betrayed; 2019, the withdrawal that handed their Syrian territory to — of all actors — Erdoğan. A fourth entry in that ledger would not be a diplomatic embarrassment; it would be a national sin of the recidivist kind, the kind Scripture says invites judgment when a nation's word to the vulnerable becomes worthless (Ezekiel 17 spends a chapter on covenant-breaking statecraft). But notice what we may rightly do without resolving the condition — because the single-witness threshold licenses exactly this: demand the inquiry. The claim is sourced to named categories of officials; it concerns the integrity of the government's most sensitive processes; Congress possesses oversight power for precisely this case. We do not need certainty to insist that those whose job is certainty go get it. And the repair obligation stands on tier-one ground regardless of the leak: the plan is exposed, the Kurds' expectations were raised, and the administration owes them either recommitment or an honest release — see question one. The judgment: pursue the inquiry lawfully and loudly; pursue the repair immediately and unconditionally.

Fourth: is this instead a brilliant stratagem — the 4D-chess defense? The claim deserves a fair hearing, so state its best form: that the MOU buys time and low oil prices while Israel dismantles the proxies; that restraining Turkey from the war, by whatever lever, prevented a catastrophic second front; that the visible humiliations are the price of a deeper repositioning not yet disclosed. Perhaps. But a strategy that cannot be distinguished from folly by any visible evidence has placed itself under the Lord's own epistemic standard: by their fruits ye shall know them (Matthew 7:16). The visible fruits to date, on tier-one and tier-two ground: an IRGC preserved, funded, and emboldened; a strait attacked with token response and a deterrent visibly eroding; a lifeline to Hezbollah cut off only when Israel and Lebanon went around the American process; an ally openly preparing to stand alone; and the message, legible to every dictator watching, that surviving an American war is a negotiating strategy. The judgment, rendered provisionally as all judgments of statecraft must be: on the fruits now visible, folly is the better-supported hypothesis than genius, and the burden of proof lies on the strategy's defenders — a burden discharged by fruits, not by assurances. If the fruits change, this council will revise its finding, and gladly. That is not weakness; provisional judgment firmly acted upon is how all statecraft and all faith operate — faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen (Hebrews 11:1) describes people who acted, decisively, without the archive in hand.

VI. The Sovereignty Floor

Beneath the deliberation, the ground that makes deliberation sane rather than desperate. Amir says in passing that everything now happening — including the parts he finds disgraceful — happens because God allows it, and that leaders are used by God for purposes they do not share. That is the Bible's explicit doctrine of history: the king's heart is in the hand of the LORD... he turneth it whithersoever he will (Proverbs 21:1); he removeth kings, and setteth up kings (Daniel 2:21); why do the heathen rage... He that sitteth in the heavens shall laugh (Psalm 2:1,4); declaring the end from the beginning... My counsel shall stand (Isaiah 46:10). Ezekiel compresses the mechanism into one image — hooks in Gog's jaws: the coalition will assemble believing it acts from its own ambitions, and every ambition will be a hook. Conscious Point Physics gives this its ontological floor: if reality is the lattice of Conscious Points processed Moment by Moment by the Mind of God, then prophecy is not prediction from outside an uncertain system — it is declaration by the One who computes every Moment, in whom every actor lives, and moves, and has his being (Acts 17:28), whose orchestration of circumstance requires the violation of no man's will. And the same ontology grounds Section II: in a universe sustained by the God who cannot lie (Titus 1:2), deception is warfare against the structure of the real — which is why an industrial deception apparatus is not merely a political fact but a spiritual one, and why every claim we verify before repeating is a small act of worship. This floor is also why the sons of Issachar can deliberate without trembling: our judgments are provisional, our action is required, and the outcome was never in our hands to begin with. We are responsible for faithfulness, not for history. History has an Author.

VII. What the Fellowship Does

So — Susan, Charlie, Leonard, Michael, Armond, and all who read these essays — the council's findings come to the floor, and honesty about our levers is the first act of seriousness: a fellowship in Montana does not move fleets. But Zechariah 4:10 forbids despising the day of small things, and our levers are real.

Pray with names in your mouth. Not atmospheric prayer — specific prayer. For the Iranian people and the Kurds, that the God who broke Pharaoh would break the IRGC and that no brave soul rises only to be abandoned. For Secretary Rubio by name, and for the administration — for kings, and for all that are in authority (1 Timothy 2:2) does not exempt administrations we distrust; pray that Rubio-shaped courage multiplies and that folly is turned back, for the King of kings turns the king's heart like a watercourse. For the peace of Jerusalem (Psalm 122:6). And for the captured on every side of this board — the Iranian conscript, the Turkish citizen taught to hate a nation that never threatened him, the Lebanese villager whose home was hollowed into a fortress over his head, the Gazan child ruled by men who built bunkers under his hospital. Our settled discipline: hate the evil; love the captured.

Speak where speech reaches power. Two specific asks, this month, to our members of Congress, in our own words on paper: oppose the F-35 transfer to Turkey while its president's annihilation rhetoric stands, and open the oversight inquiry into the leak allegation — not as verdict, but as the investigation a single credible witness has always warranted. This is precisely the infrastructure the Christos Voting Network was built for: a congregation that knows what it asks and asks it together. Add a third, positive ask: public clarity on the Kurdish question — commitment or honest release, no more encouragement without covenant.

Form the discernment this essay practiced. Teach the assertion/action distinction and the tier discipline in the fellowship, so that we are neither the simple who believe every word nor the paralyzed who sow nothing for watching the wind — a congregation that can tell moral courage from moral theater will reward the former with its voice and votes, and that, multiplied across ten thousand congregations, is how a nation's character turns.

And guard the watcher's soul. Amir confesses in this very update that the news cycle sickened him into stepping back from constant posting — the honest testimony of a professional watchman in the digital age, and the confirming exhibit for Luke's double command: look up, and lift up your heads; for your redemption draweth nigh (21:28), and six verses later, take heed to yourselves, lest at any time your hearts be overcharged... with cares of this life (21:34). Watch enough to be sober; not so much that the watching becomes the drunkenness. The believer's information diet should leave him looking like my peace I give unto you (John 14:27), not like the world's doom-scroll with a prophecy chart stapled to it — because the deliberations of Section V are the work of a settled heart, and a heart inflamed cannot sit on the council of Issachar.

The grass withereth, the flower fadeth: but the word of our God shall stand for ever (Isaiah 40:8). Amir opened his update with that verse over the news of one week. It will still be standing over the last week of the age — and over every provisional judgment this council has rendered in the meantime, correcting what we got wrong, and vindicating, we trust, that we did not wait for certainty to love righteousness out loud.
