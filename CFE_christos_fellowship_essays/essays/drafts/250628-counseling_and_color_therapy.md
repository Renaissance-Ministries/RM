**Counseling and Color Therapy**
by Thomas Lee Abshier, ND
5/9/2025

- My counseling approach helps resolve the reactivity associated with dysfunctional behavior by first identifying internal motivations and justifications.
- I emphasize the importance of understanding the emotional roots of actions, such as feelings of unfairness, unmet needs, or retaliation for inflicted pain.
- The counseling portion of the intervention is conventional and includes
  - Intake, name, age, work, marital status, medical conditions
  - Current emotional pain and circumstances stimulating the pain
  - History of the development of this condition
  - Identify the emotional reaction that will not resolve
  - If possible, identify the rationalization/justification for this emotional state and staying in it
- Treat the chronic emotional state using color therapy
  - Diffuse the associated negative emotions
  - Replace the dysfunctional reactivity with positive relational behaviors and reactions.
- Explore memories and thoughts to discharge additional reactivity in the emotional chains linked to the behavior, thereby generalizing the new functional state.
- This counseling and color therapy intervention aims to neutralize the emotional charge driving dysfunctional behavior and replace this reactivity and their associated behaviors with healthy relational responses.

**Counseling Steps**

- Analyze the bad behavior and identify its circumstances and justification.
- Use color therapy to resolve the emotional charge associated with the circumstances.
- Identify and deprogram other emotional chains associated with the behavior.
- Replace dysfunctional habits with positive behaviors.

**Counseling Approach and Justifications for Bad Behavior**

- This counseling program focuses on replacing dysfunctional behaviors with good/Godly behavior.
- This therapy is based upon the postulates that bad behavior may begin as:
  - Learned/modeled behaviors from family of origin and life lessons
  - Visceral/emotional reactions arising reflexively in life circumstances with various types of unmet needs
  - Trauma-based reactivity: acting out feelings in circumstances similar to those with past acute emotional pain or physical trauma.
- The emotion may be various patterns of victimization that justify/rationalize bad behavior, such as feelings of unfairness, lack of desired treatment, and unmet needs.
- He mentions that people often act badly due to a desire for payback, attention, or a sense of not being heard or appreciated.
- His treatment approach involves identifying the circumstances and stimuli that trigger bad behavior and the justifications that keep it in place.

**Deprogramming Bad Behavior and Emotional Chains**

- Dr Abshier includes in his process of deprogramming bad behavior the identification of the emotional justifications behind it.
- He emphasizes the importance of addressing the emotional charge associated with it and uses color therapy to break the emotional drives that stimulate repeating dysfunctional/anti-relational behavior.
- The treatment involves finding circumstances in which the behavior was deeply felt and processing the memories, thoughts, emotions, and body sensations associated with it while doing color therapy.
- He then asks the client to identify the good, functional, or Godly behavior he/she would like to incorporate as a habitual response to life circumstances.
- He asks the client to imagine the feelings, thoughts, and sensations associated with doing the functional/good/Godly behavior while doing the color therapy.
- This process releases the feelings and compulsions to act out the dysfunctional behavior. It allows the client to incorporate the new good/relational behavior.
- This process of deprogramming automated reactivity is repeated as the emotional chains related to the behavior are identified while exploring memories and current circumstances.
- Ongoing analysis and therapy are recommended to maintain these functional reactions in the face of new circumstances, develop new skills, and process feelings as new life circumstances arise.
