## Article I — Purpose

This policy is intended to protect the interests of Ideomotion and Renaissance Ministries when contemplating any transaction or arrangement that might benefit the private interest of an officer, director, manager, member, key employee, or other Interested Person (defined below) of either organization, or might result in a possible "excess benefit transaction" within the meaning of applicable federal tax law as it pertains to organizations exempt under Internal Revenue Code §501(c)(3) and §508(c)(1)(A).

This policy is intended to supplement, not replace, any applicable state and federal laws governing conflicts of interest applicable to nonprofit and charitable organizations and their affiliated subsidiaries.

---

## Article II — Definitions

**Interested Person.** Any person who has a direct or indirect Financial Interest, as defined below, in a transaction or arrangement under consideration by Ideomotion or by Renaissance Ministries on behalf of Ideomotion. Interested Persons specifically include:

- Charlie Gutierrez and Thomas Lee Abshier, ND, in their capacities as Ideomotion's principals;
- Members of Renaissance Ministries' governing body, in their capacities as the body that exercises ultimate authority over Ideomotion as a wholly-owned subsidiary;
- Any other officer, manager, employee, or contractor of Ideomotion or Renaissance Ministries who has voting or approval authority over a transaction or arrangement under consideration;
- Family members of any of the foregoing (spouse, parent, child, sibling, or any person sharing a household), and any business in which any of the foregoing or their family members holds a controlling or substantial interest.

**Financial Interest.** A person has a Financial Interest if the person has, directly or indirectly, through business, investment, or family:

(a) an ownership or investment interest in any entity with which Ideomotion or Renaissance Ministries has a transaction or arrangement;

(b) a compensation arrangement with Ideomotion or Renaissance Ministries, or with any entity or individual with which Ideomotion or Renaissance Ministries has a transaction or arrangement; or

(c) a potential ownership or investment interest in, or compensation arrangement with, any entity or individual with which Ideomotion or Renaissance Ministries is negotiating a transaction or arrangement.

Compensation includes direct and indirect remuneration as well as gifts or favors that are not insubstantial.

A Financial Interest is not necessarily a Conflict of Interest. Under Article III, Section 2, a person who has a Financial Interest may have a Conflict of Interest only if the appropriate governing body or committee decides that a Conflict of Interest exists.

## Article III — Procedures

### Section 1. Duty to disclose

In connection with any actual or possible Conflict of Interest, an Interested Person must disclose the existence of the Financial Interest and be given the opportunity to disclose all material facts to the directors and members of committees with governing-body-delegated authority who are considering the proposed transaction or arrangement.

For Ideomotion, "the appropriate body considering the transaction" is `[RM-GOV: identify the body — e.g., Renaissance Ministries' governing council; Ideomotion's manager; the RM Board acting in its capacity as Ideomotion's sole member]`. The intake form `templates/RM_governance_intake.md` exists to surface and document the answer.

### Section 2. Determining whether a Conflict of Interest exists

After disclosure of the Financial Interest and all material facts, and after any discussion with the Interested Person, the Interested Person shall leave the meeting (or otherwise recuse from the decision) while the determination of a Conflict of Interest is discussed and voted upon. The remaining body members shall decide whether a Conflict of Interest exists.

### Section 3. Procedures for addressing the Conflict of Interest

(a) An Interested Person may make a presentation at the relevant meeting, but after the presentation shall leave the meeting (or otherwise recuse) during the discussion and vote on the transaction or arrangement involving the possible Conflict of Interest.

(b) The chairperson of the body considering the matter shall, if appropriate, appoint a disinterested person or committee to investigate alternatives to the proposed transaction or arrangement.

(c) After exercising due diligence, the body shall determine whether Ideomotion (or Renaissance Ministries on its behalf) can obtain a more advantageous transaction or arrangement with reasonable efforts from a person or entity that would not give rise to a Conflict of Interest.

(d) If a more advantageous transaction or arrangement is not reasonably possible under circumstances not producing a Conflict of Interest, the body shall determine by a majority vote of the disinterested members whether the transaction or arrangement is in Ideomotion's (and Renaissance Ministries') best interest, for its own benefit, and whether the transaction is fair and reasonable. In conformity with the above determination, it shall make its decision as to whether to enter into the transaction or arrangement.

### Section 4. Violations of the Conflict-of-Interest Policy

(a) If the body has reasonable cause to believe an Interested Person has failed to disclose actual or possible Conflicts of Interest, it shall inform the Interested Person of the basis for such belief and afford the Interested Person an opportunity to explain the alleged failure to disclose.

(b) If, after hearing the Interested Person's response and after making further investigation as warranted by the circumstances, the body determines the Interested Person has failed to disclose an actual or possible Conflict of Interest, it shall take appropriate disciplinary and corrective action.

## Article IV — Records of proceedings

The minutes of the body considering any transaction or arrangement under this policy shall contain:

(a) the names of the persons who disclosed or otherwise were found to have a Financial Interest in connection with an actual or possible Conflict of Interest, the nature of the Financial Interest, any action taken to determine whether a Conflict of Interest was present, and the body's decision as to whether a Conflict of Interest in fact existed; and

(b) the names of the persons who were present for discussions and votes relating to the transaction or arrangement, the content of the discussion, including any alternatives to the proposed transaction or arrangement, and a record of any votes taken in connection therewith.

These minutes are retained as part of the organization's permanent records.

## Article V — Compensation

### Section 1. Voting members and compensation

A voting member of the governing body who receives compensation, directly or indirectly, from Ideomotion or from Renaissance Ministries in respect of services to either organization is precluded from voting on matters pertaining to that member's compensation.

### Section 2. Voting committee members and compensation

A voting member of any committee whose jurisdiction includes compensation matters and who receives compensation, directly or indirectly, from Ideomotion or Renaissance Ministries is precluded from voting on matters pertaining to that member's compensation.

### Section 3. Members receiving compensation may provide information

No voting member of the governing body or any committee whose jurisdiction includes compensation matters and who receives compensation, directly or indirectly, from Ideomotion or Renaissance Ministries is prohibited from providing information to any committee regarding compensation.

### Section 4. Rebuttable-presumption procedure for compensation

For each compensation arrangement with an Interested Person — including but not limited to compensation paid to Charlie Gutierrez and Thomas Lee Abshier, ND, by Ideomotion or by Renaissance Ministries in respect of services to either organization — the body approving the compensation shall follow the rebuttable-presumption procedure modeled on Treasury Regulations §53.4958-6:

(a) **Approval by an authorized body** composed entirely of individuals who do not have a Conflict of Interest with respect to the compensation arrangement;

(b) **Reliance on appropriate comparability data** prior to approval, including (where reasonably available) compensation levels paid by similar organizations for functionally comparable positions, current compensation surveys compiled by independent firms, and actual written offers from similar institutions competing for the services of the compensated person;

(c) **Adequate and contemporaneous documentation** of the basis for the determination, recorded in minutes that are reviewed and approved by the body within a reasonable time after the determination.

The applicability of §53.4958-6 to compensation arrangements paid by a 508(c)(1)(A) church or by its disregarded-entity subsidiary is among the questions for counsel review (`IDM_charter.md` §13). In the meantime, this policy adopts the rebuttable-presumption procedure as a matter of best practice regardless of strict legal applicability.

## Article VI — Annual statements

Each Interested Person shall annually sign a statement that affirms the person:

(a) has received a copy of the conflict-of-interest policy;
(b) has read and understands the policy;
(c) has agreed to comply with the policy; and
(d) understands that Ideomotion is a tax-aligned subsidiary of a tax-exempt religious organization and that, in order to maintain the appropriate exemption posture, the organization must engage primarily in activities that accomplish one or more of its tax-exempt purposes.

A current annual statement is retained for each Interested Person as part of the organization's permanent records.

---

## Article VII — Periodic reviews

To ensure Ideomotion operates in a manner consistent with its mission-aligned purposes and that it does not engage in activities that could jeopardize the appropriate tax posture of Renaissance Ministries, periodic reviews shall be conducted at least annually. The periodic reviews shall, at a minimum, include the following subjects:

(a) Whether compensation arrangements and benefits are reasonable, based on competent survey information, and the result of arm's-length bargaining;

(b) Whether partnerships, joint ventures, and arrangements with management organizations conform to Ideomotion's written policies, are properly recorded, reflect reasonable investment or payments for goods and services, further the organization's mission-aligned purposes, and do not result in inurement, impermissible private benefit, or in an excess benefit transaction;

(c) Whether ministry-related interactions with customers (testimony solicitation, fellowship invitation, prayer engagement) conform to the operational ethics commitments in `IDM_charter.md` §7 and the clinical and engineering integrity commitments in `IDM_charter.md` §8.

---

## Article VIII — Use of outside experts

When conducting the periodic reviews provided for in Article VII, Ideomotion may, but need not, use outside advisors. If outside experts are used, their use shall not relieve the governing body of its responsibility for ensuring that periodic reviews are conducted.
