# Renaissance Ministries — Governance Structure Intake

**Status:** TEMPLATE / WORKING DOCUMENT
**Purpose:** Surface and document the actual governance structure of Renaissance Ministries, in support of (a) the v0.3 IDM_charter.md governance-authority commitment in §9, (b) the Open Question Q9 in IDM_charter.md §12 ("what is RM's actual governance structure?"), (c) the counsel question §13.10 in IDM_charter.md, and (d) the `[RM-GOV]` placeholders in `IDM_ideomotion_ministry/IDM_conflict_of_interest_and_compensation_policy.md` and `IDM_ideomotion_ministry/IDM_operating_agreement_skeleton.md`.

**How to use:** Thomas (and where appropriate, members of the existing governing body or fellowship) fills in each section with the actual current state. "Not yet documented" or "Not yet established" are valid answers — gaps are useful information, not failures. The completed intake becomes the basis for (1) drafting or refining RM's bylaws, organizing documents, or governance charter, and (2) supplying counsel with the factual information needed to address §13 of IDM_charter.md.

**Output:** Once completed, the intake answers (or a derived "Renaissance Ministries Governance Description" document) replace the `[RM-GOV: ...]` placeholders in the operational documents. The intake itself remains as a working artifact in `templates/`.

## Use of completed intake

Once Sections 1–12 are answered, the next steps are:

1. Counsel review of the completed intake against the §13 questions in `IDM_charter.md`. Counsel may identify additional information needed before any of the proposed structure (LLC formation, subsidiary registration, COI policy adoption, etc.) can be acted on.

2. Drafting (with counsel) of formal organizing documents and bylaws for Renaissance Ministries, where currently informal or undocumented. This may be a small refinement or a substantial buildout depending on what the intake surfaces.

3. Replacement of `[RM-GOV: ...]` placeholders in the COI policy and the IDM operating agreement skeleton with concrete references to the now-documented authority structure.

4. Adoption of the COI policy and the operating agreement by the appropriate body, with proper meeting, vote, and minutes per the documented governance.

5. Filing/registering as required (state nonprofit corporation registration, charitable solicitation registration, EIN updates if needed, state subsidiary registration where the LLC is formed).

The completed intake itself, once stable, can be retained as a working reference document. The operative governance documentation lives in the formal bylaws / organizing documents themselves, not in this intake.
