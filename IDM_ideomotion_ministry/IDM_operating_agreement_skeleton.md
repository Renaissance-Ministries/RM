# Ideomotion LLC — Operating Agreement Skeleton

**Status:** SKELETON / OUTLINE — not a draft operating agreement, and not a substitute for one. This document identifies the sections an Ideomotion operating agreement will need to contain, marks what is standard for single-member LLCs owned by tax-exempt religious organizations, marks what depends on the answers to `templates/RM_governance_intake.md`, and marks what specifically requires the input of qualified nonprofit/church counsel and qualified state corporate-law counsel.

**Purpose of this skeleton:** to give Thomas, Charlie, and counsel a structured starting point — not to substitute for counsel's actual drafting work. The operating agreement is the document that gives the LLC its internal rules; in a single-member LLC owned by a religious organization, it is also the document that establishes the mission alignment between subsidiary and parent that supports the structural and tax positioning described in `IDM_charter.md` §9.

**Companion documents:**
- `IDM_charter.md` (governing mission, purpose, methods, ethics, organizational structure)
- `IDM_conflict_of_interest_and_compensation_policy.md` (referenced in Article XI)
- `templates/RM_governance_intake.md` (answers fill the `[RM-GOV]` placeholders)
- `IDM_external_description.md` (consistent external-facing summary)

## Article III — Purpose

**§3.1 Purpose.** The Company is organized exclusively to operate as a mission-aligned subsidiary of Renaissance Ministries (the "Parent Ministry"), a tax-exempt religious organization, in furtherance of the Parent Ministry's religious and charitable purposes. The specific purposes of the Company are:

(a) to design, manufacture, sell, fit, train on, and support mobility-assistance devices for individuals whose physical mobility has been compromised by injury, illness, age, or disability;

(b) to provide rehabilitative practice and support consistent with appropriate professional standards;

(c) to make voluntary spiritual care, fellowship invitation, prayer, testimony, and pastoral conversation available to customers who desire them, on the strict terms set out in §3.2 below; and

(d) to direct the net economic benefit of the Company's activities to the religious and charitable purposes of the Parent Ministry rather than to private financial benefit of the Member, the Manager, the principals, or any other Interested Person.

**§3.2 Conduct of mission-aligned activities.** The Company shall conduct its activities in accordance with the operational ethics commitments described in `IDM_charter.md` §7 (Ethics, dignity, and non-coercion) and the clinical and engineering integrity commitments described in `IDM_charter.md` §8. Specifically, no person shall be denied products or services based on religious belief or non-belief; no spiritual participation shall be a condition of receiving products or services; and no testimony or marketing material shall be used without informed, documented, revocable written consent from the participant.

**§3.3 Limitation on activities.** The Company shall not engage in any activity not in furtherance of the purposes set forth in §3.1, and shall not engage in any activity that would jeopardize the Parent Ministry's status as a tax-exempt religious organization. `[TAX-COUNSEL: review for UBIT-management language; specifically, whether and how to limit the Company's activities to those that are "substantially related" to the Parent Ministry's exempt purposes within the meaning of IRC §513, given the counsel question in IDM_charter §13.3]`.

## Article VII — Management

**§7.1 Manager-managed.** The Company is manager-managed. `[GOVERNANCE-DECISION: alternate option is member-managed; manager-managed is generally cleaner for a single-member LLC owned by an organization, because it cleanly separates the Company's operational management from the Member's authority]`.

**§7.2 Initial Manager.** The initial Manager(s) of the Company are `[name(s) — likely Charlie Gutierrez and Thomas Lee Abshier, ND, jointly or as designated]`.

**§7.3 Authority of Manager(s).** Subject to the limitations set forth in §7.4, the Manager(s) shall have full authority to manage the day-to-day operations of the Company, including without limitation the authority to:

(a) enter into contracts on behalf of the Company in the ordinary course of business;

(a) any amendment to this Operating Agreement or to the Company's Articles of Organization;
(b) any merger, consolidation, sale of substantially all assets, or dissolution of the Company;
(c) admission of any additional member;
(d) approval of compensation to a Manager or to any Interested Person, including determination of whether the rebuttable-presumption procedure of `IDM_conflict_of_interest_and_compensation_policy.md` Article V has been satisfied;
(e) incurring indebtedness or guarantees in excess of `[amount — STATE-COUNSEL]`;
(f) entering into any transaction with an Interested Person to which the conflict-of-interest policy applies;

(h) approval of the annual budget;

**§7.5 Standard of conduct.** The Manager(s) shall manage the Company in good faith, with the care that an ordinarily prudent person in like position would exercise under similar circumstances, and in a manner that the Manager(s) reasonably believe to be in the best interests of the Company and consistent with the religious and charitable purposes of the Parent Ministry. ## Article X — Books, records, and reporting

**§10.1 Books and records.** The Company shall maintain complete and accurate books and records of its activities, including (a) financial records sufficient to determine income, expenses, assets, and liabilities; (b) records of all transactions with Interested Persons; (c) minutes of any formal proceedings of the Manager(s) or Member; and (d) all governance documents (this Agreement, the Articles of Organization, the conflict-of-interest policy, periodic-review documentation under Article VII).

**§10.2 Inspection.** The Member, acting through the body or officer designated in §5.2, shall have the right to inspect the books and records of the Company at any reasonable time.

## Notes for counsel

This skeleton:

1. **Is not a draft operating agreement.** It is a structured starting point for one. Several articles contain placeholders that depend on (a) the answers to `templates/RM_governance_intake.md`, (b) the answers from qualified nonprofit/church counsel and tax counsel, and (c) the answers from state corporate-law counsel for the chosen state of formation.

2. **Adopts standard single-member LLC structure** with manager-managed governance, member reserved matters, and a charitable-dedication clause on dissolution. None of these choices is irrevocable; the skeleton can be restructured if counsel recommends a different approach (e.g., a member-managed structure if the Manager-vs-Member distinction creates more friction than value, or a different reserved-matter list, or a different signing-authority threshold).

3. **Cross-references the conflict-of-interest policy** rather than restating it inline, so that updates to the policy do not require re-execution of the operating agreement.

4. **Builds the mission-alignment language into Article III** (Purpose) so that the Parent Ministry's exempt purposes are part of the entity's organizing constitution rather than a marketing claim layered on top. This is the structural mechanism by which the IDM_charter §2 commitment ("the ministry character of Ideomotion is real rather than only declared") is realized at the entity level.

5. **Defers all UBIT, integrated-auxiliary, and disregarded-entity questions** to counsel via the `[TAX-COUNSEL: ...]` markers in Articles III, V, VI, XII, XIII, and XIV. None of these questions can be answered by the operating agreement itself; they require the application of federal tax law to the Company's actual activity mix and to the Parent Ministry's actual exempt-organization status.

6. **Defers all state-specific provisions** to state corporate-law counsel via `[STATE-COUNSEL: ...]` markers. The chosen state of formation will substantially affect Articles I, II, IX, XII, and XVI.

7. **Defers governance-specific items** to the answers from `templates/RM_governance_intake.md` via `[RM-GOV: ...]` and `[GOVERNANCE-DECISION: ...]` markers in Articles V, VII, VIII, XIII, and XV.

When the placeholders are resolved, this skeleton becomes the basis for counsel's drafting of the actual operating agreement, executed by the appropriate signatories.
