# The Alignment and the Watchman: Amir Tsarfati's June 30 Update, the Ezekiel Horizon, and How the Fellowship Should Hold Prophetic News

*Fellowship Discussion Essay | 3 July 2026*

**Occasion.** On June 30, 2026, Amir Tsarfati released one of his periodic video updates through his ministry, Behold Israel (https://beholdisrael.org). Tsarfati is an Israeli believer, a former IDF officer, and one of the most widely followed Bible-prophecy teachers in the world. His updates are valuable to us for a reason that has nothing to do with whether we share his eschatological system — we do not, in one important respect, and I will address that difference squarely in Section IV. His updates are valuable because he is an informed Israeli reading Israeli sources, hearing Israeli officials, and watching his own nation's war from inside it, and because he does what almost no secular analyst does: he asks what the events mean against the map the Hebrew prophets drew twenty-six centuries ago. The forty-four minutes of this update cover the American memorandum of understanding with Iran, a new Israel–Lebanon agreement brokered by Secretary of State Rubio, the demolition of Hezbollah's underground fortifications, Iran's leverage over the Strait of Hormuz, and Turkey's hardening posture toward Israel — and Tsarfati closes by locating all of it on the Ezekiel 38 horizon, calling the moment "a step closer to the Ezekiel war."

That closing claim is the one this essay exists to examine. Is he right? What does the text of Ezekiel 38–39 actually say, what do the current alignments actually look like against it, what may we responsibly conclude, and — the question underneath all the others — what is the faithful posture of a fellowship of believers toward news of this kind? I commend the update to you; watch it at his ministry site before or after reading this. What follows engages it in four movements: what Amir reports, how we should weigh what he reports, what the Ezekiel text itself says, and where our fellowship's eschatology of endurance places all of it. The essay closes pastorally, because prophetic news has a way of doing to the soul exactly what Amir confesses, near the top of his own update, that the news cycle had done to his: it made him sick. There is a way to watch that does not make the watchman sick, and finding it is the real deliverable of this essay.

## I. What Amir Reports

Let me first lay out his account fairly, in his framing, so that the fellowship has the substance before I begin weighing it. Everything in this section is Tsarfati's report and Tsarfati's assessment; I flag it as such because Section II will sort it into evidentiary tiers.

**The memorandum of understanding with Iran.** The United States negotiated a memorandum of understanding with Iran — first talks at Lake Lucerne in Switzerland, signature at Versailles. Amir's judgment of it is scathing. He argues it is worse than Munich in 1938, because Chamberlain at least appeased Hitler *before* a world war had demonstrated what Hitler was, whereas this MOU was signed *after* a forty-day war in which Iran was beaten to the floor — and it leaves the IRGC in power, restores its oxygen and its billions, and (this is the point he says Israel found intolerable) it treats Iran as the guardian of Lebanon, obligating Israel to withdraw from southern Lebanon and thereby throwing a lifeline to Hezbollah. He attributes the deal's shape to money — business entanglements between figures in the administration and Qatar, including the gifted aircraft now serving as Air Force One — rather than to strategic reasoning. That attribution of motive is his inference, and I will treat it as such.

**The Israel–Lebanon agreement.** Against the MOU he sets what he regards as its sane opposite: a fourteen-point agreement between Israel and the elected Lebanese government, brokered by Secretary of State Marco Rubio — whom Amir names as the one member of the current administration he trusts. On his account, the Lebanese government concluded that it fears Iranian guardianship more than it fears Israeli presence, recognized that Israel has no territorial claim on Lebanon, and signed — for the first time in history — a document permitting the IDF to remain in southern Lebanon as long as any armed militia other than the Lebanese Armed Forces operates there. Since Hezbollah refuses to disarm, the practical meaning is that Israel stays until Hezbollah is dismantled. The structure, he notes, mirrors Gaza: presence until the terror organization is gone, not withdrawal on a promise.

**The underground war.** He shows footage of the demolition of an enormous Hezbollah facility beneath the village of Majdal Zoun in southern Lebanon — a fortification some forty-five meters underground, with rocket launchers, drone-assembly rooms, and command centers built beneath civilian homes, whose destruction split the village and left a crater at its heart. He reports similar facilities elsewhere, including a missile complex and another site where Hezbollah and Iranian officers are said to be trapped underground with the IDF sitting on top of it. In Gaza, he reports the destruction of a ten-mile tunnel system on the Egyptian border, neutralized by pouring thirty thousand cubic meters of concrete — and he notes wryly that the concrete emerging from hidden shafts into houses and streets is how you find the shafts.

**Iran's remaining weapon.** With its nuclear program set back and its proxies being ground down, Iran's leverage, on Amir's reading, is now the Strait of Hormuz — a chokepoint it has attacked repeatedly in recent days with only token American response. He argues Iran has every incentive to humiliate the administration through the midterm elections, and that the MOU perversely handed Iran a tripwire: any Israeli action in Lebanon can now be framed as an MOU violation justifying closure of the strait. A regime claiming the right to close an international waterway, he observes, is a precedent the world cannot live with — imagine Morocco closing Gibraltar.

**Turkey.** The report's most sobering thread. Amir plays a clip of the President remarking, in the presence of a Turkish journalist, that Erdogan "stayed out of the war" — which Amir reads as an unguarded admission that Turkey nearly entered the recent war on Iran's side, against Israel, and was talked out of it. He further reports — citing an Israeli general, a broad hint from the Israeli Defense Minister, and an Emirati official — a claim that the Mossad had prepared a plan to arm the Kurds and other Iranian minorities to topple the regime, and that Vice President Vance leaked the plan to Erdogan, who intervened with the President to smother it. He reports Erdogan's recent declaration that Zionism has set its sights on Turkey itself and that Turkey fights for its survival — rhetoric Amir reads as ground-preparation for eventual confrontation — and Netanyahu's cabinet response that when someone declares his intent to annihilate you, the history of the Jewish people teaches you to take him seriously. He notes that Israel, days ago, formally recognized the Armenian genocide, a moral declaration aimed squarely at Ankara. And he reports that Israel is now actively preparing its defenses for a war it expects to fight alone, having concluded that American help cannot be assumed.

**The information war.** He reports that a statistician (he places him at the University of Pennsylvania) has analyzed the Gaza casualty figures — all of which originate with the Hamas-run health ministry — and found them internally incoherent, with civilian-category tallies inflated and all deaths, whatever their cause, reported as war deaths; and he claims the famine photographs that circled the world were, in significant cases, children with genetic diseases photographed without that context, sometimes beside healthy siblings. His conclusion: no genocide, no engineered starvation — a war against an enemy embedded under mosques, schools, and hospitals, with a civilian toll he characterizes as low by the standards of urban warfare.

**The horizon.** And then the closing move that frames everything: Iran emboldened and funded; Turkey positioning itself against Israel and lying about Israel's intentions; Russia already estranged; Libya and Sudan in the wings. To Amir this is the Ezekiel 38 coalition assembling in real time, and the moment is, in his words, a step closer to that war.

## II. Weighing the Report: The Ninth Commandment Applied to News We Are Inclined to Believe

Before we take up Ezekiel, we owe the report itself an honest audit — and here I want the fellowship to notice something beautiful in Amir's own update. He opens it in prayer, and the burden of the prayer is deception: he asks God for right information in an ocean of lies, and he anchors the request in Isaiah 40:8 — *The grass withereth, the flower fadeth: but the word of our God shall stand for ever.* That prayer binds him, and it binds us. The discipline it names does not apply only to the other side's propaganda. It applies with special force to reports we are *inclined* to believe, because inclination is precisely where discernment goes to sleep. We built this discipline in the Thirty-Eight Firsts essay: the ninth commandment — *Thou shalt not bear false witness* (Exodus 20:16) — governs not only the lies we originate but the unverified claims we repeat, and *He that is first in his own cause seemeth just; but his neighbour cometh and searcheth him* (Proverbs 18:17).

So let us sort Amir's report into three tiers, not to diminish him but to obey the commandment.

**Tier one: public, verifiable events.** The existence of the MOU and where it was negotiated and signed; the existence and published points of the Israel–Lebanon agreement; the demolition at Majdal Zoun, which Israel filmed and released; the Gaza tunnel destruction and the concrete; Erdogan's speeches, which are on the record; Netanyahu's cabinet statement, which he quotes in full; Israel's recognition of the Armenian genocide, announced by Foreign Minister Sa'ar; Iran's attacks on shipping and its refusal to attend talks. These are checkable against primary sources, and the fellowship may hold them with confidence proportionate to that checking.

**Tier two: informed interpretation.** That the MOU is worse than Munich; that money and Qatari influence, rather than strategic conviction, shaped it; that Iran intends to bleed the administration through the midterms; that Erdogan's rhetoric is ground-preparation for war. These are the judgments of an intelligent, well-sourced Israeli observer with a known vantage point and known commitments. Interpretation from a good watchman is worth a great deal — and it is still interpretation. Hold it as his read, weigh it against other reads, and notice that his read of Lebanese motives, at least, is corroborated by the extraordinary fact of the Lebanese signature itself.

**Tier three: single-sourced or contested claims.** Three items belong here, and I name them without embarrassment because Amir himself names his sources and lets us see the sourcing. First, the claim that the Vice President leaked the Kurdish plan to Erdogan — sourced to an Israeli general, a ministerial hint, and an Emirati official, which is more than nothing and less than proof, and which involves an accusation of something close to betrayal against a named living man. The ninth commandment requires us to hold that one loosely until it is established in the mouth of two or three witnesses we can examine (Deuteronomy 19:15). Second, the casualty-statistics analysis: there is genuinely serious scholarly work questioning the Hamas ministry figures, and there is also a fog of motivated analysis on every side of this war; the specific claims should be traced to the specific study before they are repeated as settled. Third, the fabricated-starvation claim: documented cases exist of misleading famine photography, and it does not follow that all suffering in Gaza was staged; the honest position holds both the real manipulation and the real suffering without letting either erase the other. A fellowship that repeats tier-three claims as tier-one facts has borne false witness even if every claim later proves true, because we asserted a certainty we did not possess.

None of this is distrust of Amir. It is the discipline his own opening prayer asked God to work in his hearers. The watchman's report and the hearer's verification are two halves of one obedience.

## III. The Ezekiel Text Itself

Now to the claim that frames the whole update. Ezekiel 38 and 39 describe a war that has never yet occurred in history: a great northern coalition descending on a regathered Israel and being destroyed not by Israel's army but by God's direct intervention, for God's explicitly stated purpose of self-revelation to the nations. Because Amir's entire reading of the moment hangs on this text, the fellowship should have the text itself before it, not a summary of a summary.

The coalition is named in Ezekiel 38:1–6. *Son of man, set thy face against Gog, the land of Magog, the chief prince of Meshech and Tubal... Persia, Ethiopia, and Libya with them... Gomer, and all his bands; the house of Togarmah of the north quarters.* The timing is named twice: *in the latter years* (38:8) and *in the latter days* (38:16). The target's condition is named with equal precision: a people *brought back from the sword, and gathered out of many people, against the mountains of Israel, which have been always waste* (38:8), a people *at rest, that dwell safely, all of them dwelling without walls, and having neither bars nor gates* (38:11). The invader's motive is plunder — *to take a spoil, and to take a prey* (38:12). The divine agency is unmistakable: *I will turn thee back, and put hooks into thy jaws* (38:4) — God Himself draws the coalition down. And the purpose is stated in God's own voice: *Thus will I magnify myself, and sanctify myself; and I will be known in the eyes of many nations, and they shall know that I am the LORD* (38:23), and again, *So will I make my holy name known in the midst of my people Israel... and the heathen shall know that I am the LORD, the Holy One in Israel* (39:7).

Who are the named nations? Here the fellowship should know which identifications are solid and which are contested, because prophecy teaching in America often presents the whole list with uniform confidence, and the text deserves better.

**Persia** is the one identification requiring no argument at all. Persia is Iran — the name was not even changed until 1935. The chief financier, armer, and ideological engine of the current war against Israel is named in the text without any interpretive step whatsoever. Whatever one concludes about the rest of the list, that fact alone should arrest the attention of any honest reader.

**Meshech, Tubal, Gomer, and Togarmah** are, by the sober consensus of historical geography, peoples of ancient Anatolia — the landmass of modern Turkey. The Assyrian records know Meshech and Tubal as the Mushki and Tabal of central and eastern Anatolia; Gomer corresponds to the Cimmerians who settled there; Togarmah (Til-garimmu) lies in the Armenian highlands of eastern Turkey. This is worth pausing over, because the popular American prophecy tradition has spent seventy years training believers to hear "Meshech and Tubal" as Moscow and Tobolsk — an identification resting on phonetic resemblance and nothing else. The historically grounded reading puts *Turkey*, not Russia, at the center of the coalition's mass. And it is Turkey whose president now speaks openly of the Zionist threat to his nation's survival and of the liberation of Jerusalem. If the Anatolian identification is right, the most striking development in Amir's entire update — Erdogan's pivot from cold rival to declared existential enemy — is also the most textually significant.

**Rosh** is the contested term. The Hebrew *rosh* ordinarily means "head" or "chief," and the King James renders it so: *the chief prince of Meshech and Tubal.* Some translations, following a different parsing, render it as a proper noun, "prince of Rosh" — and on that reading the Russia identification is built. The honest position is that the Russia connection is possible but rests on a disputed translation, whereas Turkey's presence in the coalition rests on the firmest identifications in the list. Russia's participation would surprise no one watching its current alignments with Iran; but the text does not *require* Russia the way it names Persia and Anatolia.

**Ethiopia and Libya** in the KJV render Hebrew *Cush* and *Put* — the upper Nile region (modern Sudan more than modern Ethiopia) and North Africa west of Egypt (Libya). Amir names Libya and Sudan as the coalition's remaining members, and those are in fact the defensible identifications.

Now set the current moment against the text, and let me be as careful in the fitting as I was in the identifying. What fits: Israel is regathered from the nations after near-annihilation by the sword — the precondition of 38:8 that made this chapter a dead letter for eighteen centuries and a live one since 1948. Persia is Israel's declared enemy, wounded and vengeful. Turkey — the coalition's geographic core on the sound reading — has moved in a single generation from Israel's regional partner to a state whose leader publicly frames Israel's existence as a threat to his own. Sudan and Libya are broken states open to exactly the kind of client participation the text describes. And — a detail worth noticing — the coalition of Ezekiel 38 conspicuously *lacks* Israel's inner ring: no Egypt, no Jordan, no Lebanon, no entity answering to Hamas or Hezbollah. The prophecy describes an outer-ring war. And what is the news of this very update? The inner ring is being dismantled — Hezbollah's fortress-tunnels demolished, Hamas ground down, Lebanon's elected government signing with Israel, Egypt and Jordan long at peace. The battlefield the text presupposes, in which the near enemies are absent and only the far coalition remains, is being cleared before our eyes.

And what does not yet fit — this the fellowship must hold with equal firmness: Israel does not yet *dwell safely, without walls, having neither bars nor gates.* Israel in July 2026 is a nation at war, mobilized, walled, and watching every border. Some interpreters soften the phrase to mean "dwelling securely, confident in her defenses"; perhaps. But on the plain reading, a precondition of the invasion remains unfulfilled — and it is even possible that the current agreements, if they hold, are themselves the instrument by which the false security of 38:11 will one day be manufactured. That is speculation, and I mark it as speculation. What is not speculation is the Lord's own word about timing: *But of that day and that hour knoweth no man* (Mark 13:32). The pattern-fit is real and remarkable. The date is not ours to set. Watchfulness that hardens into a timetable has left the text behind.

So is Amir right that we are "a step closer"? On the alignment of the named nations — yes, and the honest historical geography makes the case *stronger* than the pop-prophecy version does, because it puts Turkey where the text puts the coalition's mass. On the nearness — the text withholds what he, and we, would like to know. Closer is defensible. *Close* is more than anyone can say.

## IV. Where We Differ from Amir: Watching to Endure, Not Watching to Leave

Now the difference, stated plainly and with love for a brother. Amir Tsarfati teaches within the dispensational, pre-tribulation-rapture system: the Church will be removed before the great trial, and the prophetic events now aligning are, for the believer, the sound of the departure platform being readied. Our fellowship, after the study laid out at length in *Endurance, Not Escape*, holds the historic eschatology of the Church — the eschatology of Irenaeus and Augustine and every century before Darby's revision in the 1830s: one return of Christ, public and glorious, after tribulation; and the saints present through the trial, kept *in* it rather than *from* it, as Christ prayed in John 17:15 — *I pray not that thou shouldest take them out of the world, but that thou shouldest keep them from the evil.*

Notice carefully what this difference does and does not touch. It does not touch the value of Amir's watching. The command to watch is not the property of any rapture position: *And what I say unto you I say unto all, Watch* (Mark 13:37). Ezekiel 38 says what it says regardless of where one places a seven-year clock, and the alignment Amir documents is the same alignment on either eschatology. A watchman on the wall reporting troop movements is performing the office of Ezekiel 33 whether or not his map of the campaign's later stages matches ours, and I receive his report with gratitude.

What the difference touches is the *purpose* of the watching — and therefore everything about how the fellowship should respond to updates like this one. The pre-trib believer watches the way a traveler watches the departures board: the events concern him as signs of his own imminent removal, and his practical preparation is to have his accounts current for the airlift. The believer of the historic eschatology watches the way a garrison watches: the events concern him as the shape of the trial *through which* he and his children and his fellowship will be asked to stand, and his practical preparation is character, community, provision, courage, and a faith that has made peace in advance with loss. This is the entire logic of what we have been calling the Christian Underground. If the nations are aligning on the Ezekiel pattern — and Section III says the alignment is real — then the endurance reading does not respond with a packed suitcase. It responds by deepening the disciplines: the fellowship bonds that hold when institutions fail, the habit of obeying God rather than men rehearsed in small things before it is demanded in great ones, the household economics of a people who may one day be squeezed, and above all the settled interior knowledge of Christ that no external emergency can improvise.

And here Amir gives us, unintentionally, the confirming exhibit. He confesses in this very update that he withdrew from constant Telegram posting because he found himself emotionally invested to the point that the news cycle was making him ill. I honor the honesty; it is the confession of every watchman in the digital age. But mark it well: if watching the signs sickens even the professional watchman — a man of faith, doing it as ministry — then a fellowship that consumes prophetic news as a spectator sport, refreshing feeds for the next development, is not preparing for anything. It is being digested by the thing it watches. The endurance eschatology carries its own medicine here, and Section VI will administer it.

## V. The Sovereignty Floor: Hooks, Kings, and the Mind That Sustains Every Moment

Beneath the geopolitics and beneath the eschatology lies the question that decides whether any of this watching is rational at all: *can* a text written twenty-six centuries ago actually constrain the behavior of nations in 2026? The secular mind says the question answers itself — pattern-matching on vague ancient poetry. The fellowship should be able to say why the secular mind is wrong, and to say it at the level of ontology, not merely of piety.

Begin with what Amir himself says in passing: that everything now happening, including the parts he finds disgraceful, is happening because God allows it, and that leaders can be used by God to advance purposes they do not share. That is not a coping sentiment; it is the Bible's explicit doctrine of history. *The king's heart is in the hand of the LORD, as the rivers of water: he turneth it whithersoever he will* (Proverbs 21:1). *He removeth kings, and setteth up kings* (Daniel 2:21). *Why do the heathen rage, and the people imagine a vain thing?... He that sitteth in the heavens shall laugh* (Psalm 2:1,4). And the claim that grounds prophecy as such: *I am God, and there is none like me, declaring the end from the beginning, and from ancient times the things that are not yet done, saying, My counsel shall stand, and I will do all my pleasure* (Isaiah 46:9–10). Ezekiel 38 makes the mechanism vivid in a single image: God puts *hooks in the jaws* of Gog. The coalition will assemble believing it acts from its own ambition, its own grievances, its own plunder-calculus — and every one of those motives will be a hook.

Conscious Point Physics gives this doctrine its ontological floor. If reality is what the framework holds it to be — a universe constituted of Conscious Points, every one of them a locus of divine awareness, the whole lattice processed Moment by Moment by the Mind of God — then prophecy is not prediction at all, not in the sense of a forecast made from outside a system whose future is uncertain. Prophecy is *declaration by the One who computes every Moment*. The God who spoke through Ezekiel is not a superintelligent observer estimating probable outcomes from initial conditions; He is the sustaining Mind within whom every initial condition, every intermediate state, and every human deliberation has its being — *for in him we live, and move, and have our being* (Acts 17:28). Human freedom is real within that processing; the hooks in Gog's jaws are not the override of Gog's will but the orchestration of the circumstances, incentives, humiliations, and ambitions to which Gog's freely evil will predictably responds. A God who sustains every Conscious Point does not need to violate a single choice to guarantee an outcome declared twenty-six centuries in advance. That is why the alignment of Persia and the Anatolian powers against a regathered Israel is not an astonishing coincidence to be explained away, and why the fellowship can watch it without vertigo. The text holds because the One who spoke it is the One in whom the actors act.

The same ontology grounds the discipline of Section II. In a conscious universe sustained by the God who *cannot lie* (Titus 1:2), truth is not a social convention; it is alignment with the actual state of the divinely processed real, and deception is a positive violation of the Logos-structure of existence — which is why Scripture gives falsehood a personal author: *he is a liar, and the father of it* (John 8:44). The ocean of misinformation Amir prays against is not noise; it is warfare against the nature of reality itself. And that is why our verification discipline is not journalistic fussiness but worship. Every claim we decline to repeat until it is established, every tier-three report we hold loosely, every correction we accept against our inclination, is a small act of alignment with the God who is Truth.

## VI. Closing: How to Watch Without Being Consumed

So, to the fellowship — Susan, Charlie, Leonard, Michael, Armond, and all who read these essays — here is where the whole update lands.

Receive the watchman's report with gratitude. Amir Tsarfati is doing, at scale and at personal cost, the work of Ezekiel 33:6 — the watchman who sees the sword coming and blows the trumpet. That his map of the last campaign differs from ours in the placement of one event does not make the trumpet less worth hearing. Read his reports; and read them with the three-tier discipline of Section II, because the ninth commandment does not adjourn when the news is exciting.

Take the Ezekiel alignment seriously, and refuse the timetable. What is forming on the northern horizon fits the text — fits it better, in fact, when the text is read with sober historical geography than when it is read through Cold War glasses. Persia is in place. The Anatolian core is turning. The inner ring is being cleared. And Israel does not yet dwell safely, and no man knows the day or the hour. Both halves of that sentence are obedience. Speak of these things to your neighbors and children the way the text itself speaks: with awe at the God who declares the end from the beginning, and with silence where He has kept silence.

Prepare as the endurance eschatology prepares. If the trial approaches, we expect to be present for it. The response to an aligning world is not a packed bag but a deepened root: the disciplines of the fellowship, the household in order, the courage rehearsed in small obediences, the Word stored where no confiscation can reach it — *thy word have I hid in mine heart* (Psalm 119:11). Everything we have been building this year — the essays, the fellowship bonds, the Underground disciplines — is the practical form of Ezekiel-watching for people who expect to stand rather than to depart.

Guard the watcher's soul. Our Lord issued both warnings in a single discourse, and most believers hear only one of them. *When these things begin to come to pass, then look up, and lift up your heads; for your redemption draweth nigh* (Luke 21:28) — that is the command to watch. And six verses later: *Take heed to yourselves, lest at any time your hearts be overcharged with surfeiting, and drunkenness, and cares of this life* (Luke 21:34) — and the cares of this life, in our age, arrive chiefly through a glowing rectangle, hourly, engineered to inflame. Amir's confession about the sickness of over-watching is the tale of a good man discovering Luke 21:34 the hard way. Set a measure on your news as you would set a measure on wine. Watch enough to be sober; not so much that the watching becomes the drunkenness. The believer's information diet should leave him looking more like *my peace I give unto you: not as the world giveth* (John 14:27) and less like the world's own doom-scrolling anxiety with a prophecy chart stapled to it.

And pray — for all of them. *Pray for the peace of Jerusalem: they shall prosper that love thee* (Psalm 122:6). Pray for rulers, including the ones whose decisions this update laments, *for kings, and for all that are in authority; that we may lead a quiet and peaceable life* (1 Timothy 2:2) — the command does not exempt administrations we distrust. And pray for the captured: the Iranian people under the IRGC's boot, the Turkish people being taught to hate a nation that has never threatened them, the Lebanese villagers whose homes were hollowed into fortresses over their heads, the Gazan families ruled by men who built their bunkers under the children's hospitals. Our settled discipline holds here as everywhere: hate the evil; love the captured. The coalition of Ezekiel 38 is, at the level that matters most, a column of captured men marching on hooks toward a judgment they cannot see — and the God who set the hooks states His purpose twice, and it is not, finally, destruction but revelation: *that the heathen may know me* (Ezekiel 38:16). The war, when it comes, is an evangelistic act on a scale no missionary movement ever dreamed. Until it comes, we are the quieter version of the same revelation — the people whose peace under pressure, whose truthfulness in an ocean of lies, and whose love for the captured make the nations ask their question early: *what manner of people are these, and who is their God?*

*The grass withereth, the flower fadeth: but the word of our God shall stand for ever* (Isaiah 40:8). Amir opened with that verse over the news of a single week. It will still be standing over the last week of the age.

— Thomas
