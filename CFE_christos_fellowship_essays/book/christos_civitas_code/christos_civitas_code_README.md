# The Christos Civitas Code — Book README

**Renaissance Ministries Book Project | First-Draft Status | April 28, 2026**

---

## What this is

This folder holds the first-draft manuscript of *The Christos Civitas Code: How to Hold Firm Convictions and Love Captured Friends in a Fractured Cultural Moment.* The book articulates the four-component relational ethic developed in the Renaissance Ministries fellowship essays of April 2026 and grounds it in the apostolic deposit, the phenomenology-of-the-Cross theological framework, and worked examples drawn from the fellowship's actual situation in 2026 American Christian life.

The book is intentionally cross-cutting and therefore lives in `CFE_christos_fellowship_essays/book/` — the Christos Fellowship Essays module — rather than in any single domain module. Its substantive content draws on:

- **CRF** (Christos Rigorous Framework): the axiom-to-theorem grounding of relational ethics in conscious-point ontology and the structure of God's experiential indwelling
- **CAP** (Christos Apologetics): the apostolic-deposit ecclesiology, the closed-canon argument, the historic creedal tradition
- **CCC** (Christos Christianity Catalog): the worked Mormonism case study used as one extended example of the framework in action
- **CPL** (Christos Political Theory): the constitutional-ordering convictions and the application of the framework to political and cultural conversations

The book is a fellowship essay that has grown into book length because the question it answers requires it. Most fellowship essays are 4,000-8,000 words and serve a single discussion. The Christos Civitas Code is approximately 50,000-65,000 words at completion and serves as a freestanding reference work.

## What this is not

This is not a polemic against the contemporary cultural left or right. It is also not a procedural-neutrality manual that asks the believer to dilute his convictions in the name of friendship. The book occupies a third space that the contemporary publishing landscape mostly fails to fill: a serious Christian articulation of how to hold firm convictions in a fractured cultural moment without becoming either soft or contemptuous.

The book is not a Christianity-versus-secularism argument. It does engage thoughtful natural-law conservatives like Dr. Rick Kirschner as fellow-travelers reaching largely the same conclusions through general revelation, and it explicitly addresses the question Kirschner raised about the fracturing of American discourse. But the book's primary audience is Christians, particularly those who feel the tension between firm conviction and loving relationship that the contemporary moment has intensified.

## Status of this manuscript

**FIRST DRAFT.** Every chapter is in draft form. The drafts are substantive — they articulate the central argument of each chapter, ground each in scriptural warrant, develop the worked examples that the framework requires, and produce a coherent whole when read in sequence. But every chapter expects editorial passes by Thomas before it serves as final book material.

The drafting pattern is: Claude produces the initial draft from the foundation supplied by the existing fellowship essays and from the framework articulated in the two relational-ethics essays of April 28, 2026. Thomas edits each chapter in his own voice, on his own schedule, and either re-submits for further Claude work or marks the chapter as ready for the next stage of the book pipeline.

This pattern matches Thomas's stated preference for editing over first-drafting. The hard part of book production is getting the ideas onto the page; once they are on the page, Thomas can shape them into final form fluently.

## Manuscript structure

The book is in three parts, eight chapters:

### Part One: The Ground

- **Chapter 1: What We Confess** — The substantive convictions of the Christos Civitas. Refuses the contemporary etiquette that treats firm conviction as itself impolite.
- **Chapter 2: The Foundation Christ Laid** — The Matthew 16:18 ecclesiology. Why the apostolic deposit is closed and the foundation has not been moved.
- **Chapter 3: Why the Cross Is Morally Coherent** — The phenomenology-of-the-Cross framework. The theological foundation on which the relational ethic of Part Two rests.

### Part Two: The Code

- **Chapter 4: Component One — Holding the Conviction Firmly** — Why the convictions of Part One are stand-line in real spiritual warfare.
- **Chapter 5: Component Two — Hating the Evil, Loving the Captured** — The substantive heart of the book. Proper direction of hatred and love.
- **Chapter 6: Component Three — Listening as Theological Discipline** — The two warrants and the four diagnostic possibilities.
- **Chapter 7: Component Four — Distinguishing Certainties, Strong Inferences, and Prudential Judgments** — The taxonomy that frees the believer from rigidity without dissolving into softness.

### Part Three: The Witness

- **Chapter 8: The Code in the Conversations We Are Actually Having** — Worked examples drawn from the fellowship's actual life. What the framework looks like when lived rather than just articulated.

## Editorial pattern

Each chapter has YAML frontmatter declaring its status. As Thomas edits a chapter, the frontmatter `status` field updates:

- `FIRST_DRAFT` — Claude's initial draft. Substantive but expected to be edited.
- `THOMAS_EDIT_PASS_1` — Thomas has done one editorial pass.
- `READY_FOR_REVIEW` — Thomas considers the chapter ready for further Claude review or for the next stage.
- `ESTABLISHED` — Thomas has accepted the chapter as final book material.

Chapters can move back and forth between statuses as the editing process unfolds. There is no expectation that chapters reach `ESTABLISHED` quickly. The book emerges from sustained editorial work, not from any single drafting session.
