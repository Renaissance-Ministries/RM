# Chapter 8: The Code in the Conversations We Are Actually Having

A framework that works only on paper is not yet a framework that works. The seven preceding chapters have established the substantive convictions, the foundation that makes the convictions non-arbitrary, the theological coherence of the Cross that the framework presupposes, and the four-component relational ethic that the framework articulates. The work of this final chapter is to take the framework into the actual conversations the fellowship is having in 2026, and to show what the framework looks like when lived rather than just articulated.

The chapter develops eight worked examples. Each is drawn from the fellowship's actual situation or from the kinds of conversations every member of the fellowship is having across various lines of disagreement. The examples are not exhaustive; the actual conversations will have their own textures the worked examples cannot anticipate. But the examples show the shape of the work and equip the reader to do the work in his own life.

The eight cases are:

1. The friend captured by a restoration-claim framework (the Leonard / Snuffer case)
2. The political conversation across family lines
3. The philosophical seeker who has built his own framework over decades (the Mike Sherman case)
4. The progressive friend who has reached opposite conclusions on the great moral questions
5. The conservative friend who has slid into contempt
6. The young believer being pressured to soften by his peer group
7. The older believer hardened into rigidity
8. The cultural moment at large

After the eight cases, the chapter closes with a brief note on what the witness is for and what it leaves to the Spirit and to time.

## Case 1: The friend captured by a restoration-claim framework

Leonard is a member of this fellowship. He left LDS Mormonism after years of careful study. He encountered Denver Snuffer's writings. He accepted re-baptism into the Snuffer movement. He believes, sincerely, that the Snuffer corpus represents a continuation of the restoration that Joseph Smith began, that the historic Christian creedal tradition is a substantial corruption of apostolic Christianity, and that what he has found in Snuffer's framework is closer to the original gospel than what most Christians today confess.

The fellowship has spent the last week working through Snuffer's published corpus systematically. Six engagement essays totaling roughly 35,000 words have been committed to the Renaissance Ministries repository. The convictions on the record: that the Snuffer framework rests on a foundation Matthew 16:18 forecloses; that the structural movement from the 2017 Cerritos address (the front porch) to the 2025 Philippines address (the closed door, all baptisms invalidated, the Trinity called a stumbling block, the historic creeds called abomination) discloses the trajectory the front porch was always pointing toward; that the Gethsemane-substitution atonement theology departs from the consistent apostolic-deposit witness placing the atonement at the Cross; that the Daniel 2 *stone cut without hands* is Christ Himself rather than the Book of Mormon. The convictions are firm. Component One has been honored.

What does the conversation with Leonard, beginning now, require?

**Component Two:** Leonard is not the Snuffer movement. Leonard is a brother captured by a framework that grieves God, and the work is to hate the framework while loving the brother. The grief over what the framework is doing in his life is appropriate. The love for Leonard himself is appropriate. The two operate simultaneously. The believer does not soften the conviction about the framework to keep the friendship comfortable; he also does not extend the conviction onto Leonard such that Leonard becomes the object of contempt rather than rescue. Leonard is loved. The framework is hated. Both at full intensity.

The phenomenology connection sharpens this. If God experiences His creatures from inside, then God is grieving over Leonard's captivity from inside Leonard right now. The grief is real. The reaching is real. The believer's love for Leonard is participation in the love God is currently extending to Leonard from inside the captivity. The believer's hatred of the Snuffer framework is hatred of the captor that has captured the brother God is loving. Both motions are aligned with what God Himself is doing.

**Component Three:** Listening first. Leonard has been on a journey, and the journey has been serious. He has paid real costs — relational costs in his LDS family, identity costs, the costs of learning a new framework with new vocabulary and new rhythms. Whatever the believer thinks of the framework, the journey is Leonard's and it has been real. The first move in the conversation is not for the believer to explain why the framework is mistaken; it is for the believer to ask Leonard what the journey has been like, what he has found in the Snuffer movement that the LDS Church did not give him, what he loves about where he is now. Leonard will tell. And he will know, by the texture of how the believer asks, whether the believer is listening or queuing rebuttals.

The four diagnostic possibilities apply across the conversation. Where Leonard names something real — that the LDS hierarchical structure he came out of had genuine corruption, that his hunger for direct contact with God is right, that the moral seriousness he found in the Snuffer movement is real — the believer can affirm. Where Leonard's frame is missing something the apostolic deposit supplies, the believer can introduce — gently, when the openings appear. Where Leonard's real spiritual energy has been mis-channeled into the restoration framework, the believer can redirect — *I see what you are reaching for; I think the form it has taken is misdirected; let me show you what the historic Christian tradition offers as a different channel for the same hunger.* Where the framework's specific claims actively contradict the apostolic deposit and the contradiction is at structural points (the necessity of new authority, the invalidity of historic Christian baptisms, the rejection of the Trinity), the believer can name what he is seeing and decline to host the spirit.

**Component Four:** The certainty is the apostolic deposit and the foundation Christ laid. The prudential judgment is how the conversation with Leonard is held over what specific time, with what specific timing of which specific topics, in what specific tone. The certainty does not change. The prudential judgment is open to the Spirit's guidance in real time. The believer is not running a script; he is engaging an actual person across actual conversations, and the form the conversations take will be improvised, prayed for, allowed to unfold.

A specific note that the fellowship has settled: Leonard's baptism into the Snuffer movement does not need to be redone for him to stand on the foundation Christ laid. The framework around the baptism is mistaken; the faith inside the framework is not invalidated. The Christ in whose name Leonard sought baptism is the Christ Peter confessed; that places Leonard on the foundation Christ laid. The Snuffer-specific claims about authority and re-baptism are theologically incorrect, but they do not have the power to invalidate Leonard's actual standing in Christ. Romans 8:9 — *if any man have not the Spirit of Christ, he is none of his.* The Spirit-of-Christ test is the test, and the test is met. This pastoral position is available to the conversation if the question of baptism arises.

The work, once the conversation begins, is sustained. Leonard may receive what is offered; he may push back; he may return at a different time with new questions; he may not return at all. The believer does not control the outcome. The believer controls the posture: firm conviction, hatred of the captivity, love of the brother, careful listening, taxonomy held straight. Over time, the witness will do what the witness does. The Spirit will do what the Spirit does. The believer's work is to remain present and faithful.

## Case 2: The political conversation across family lines

Most members of the fellowship have family members on the other side of one or more political fault lines. A spouse, sibling, child, parent, or in-law who voted differently, who watches different news sources, who has reached opposite conclusions on the cultural questions of the moment. The Thanksgiving dinner table has become difficult. The text message thread is brittle. The phone calls are short.

What does the framework require?

**Component One:** The convictions are firm. The believer does not pretend not to believe what he believes about the personhood of the unborn, the created order of marriage, the constitutional ordering, the moral seriousness of the great cultural questions. He does not perform agnosticism for the sake of family comfort. The convictions are stated when the questions arise, plainly, without aggression but also without softening.

**Component Two:** The family member is not the position. The family member is the person we have known and loved across decades, whose image-bearer status is not contingent on his political conclusions, whose relationship with us is not reducible to whether he agrees with us on the cultural questions of the moment. The family member is loved fiercely. The political position the family member holds — where it is genuinely captured by something destructive — is what is hated, not the family member. The two motions are kept separate. The contempt-culture posture that says *if you voted for that person you are dead to me* is rejected. The progressive-flattening posture that says *I will pretend to agree with you to keep the relationship* is also rejected.

**Component Three:** Listening. Family members who have reached different conclusions usually have reasons that are not stupid. They have absorbed different information environments, different framings, different first-person experiences. They are responding to real concerns even when their conclusions are mistaken. The believer who listens carefully will often find that the family member's concern is real and right, even where the conclusion has been mis-channeled. The redirect move (Component Three's third diagnostic) is often the most fruitful here. *I see what you are responding to. The thing you care about is real. I think the form your conclusion has taken misses what you actually want.*

**Component Four:** Most political-cultural disagreements are at the prudential level. The substantive certainty about the personhood of the unborn is one thing; the specific candidate to support in any specific election is quite another. The believer who has done the taxonomy work can hold his prudential judgments seriously without converting them into certainties such that the family member who has reached a different prudential conclusion becomes theologically suspect. The family relationships can survive prudential disagreement when the believer keeps the categories straight; they cannot survive when every disagreement has been elevated to a doctrinal anathema.

The texture of this case varies enormously by family. Some family members are open, engaged, willing to think; the framework produces real fruit over time. Some are closed, hostile, treating any disagreement as personal attack; the framework produces little visible fruit but preserves the relationship through what would otherwise have been a complete break. Some are in between. The believer does not control the texture. He controls the posture: firm where firmness belongs, gentle where gentleness belongs, charitable where charity belongs. The relationships are held as long as they can be held, and where they break despite faithful work, the break is on the other side, not on the believer's.

## Case 3: The philosophical seeker (Mike Sherman)

Mike Sherman is a longtime member of this fellowship. He has been working out his own philosophical-religious synthesis for fifty years. His *Meaning in Life* mandala system represents a serious, careful construction that he has refined over decades of thought, study, and writing. Mike does not confess the apostolic deposit as the historic Christian church confesses it. He has built a framework that does the work of a religion without explicitly affirming the Trinitarian God of orthodox Christianity. He is also one of the fellowship's deepest interlocutors, intellectually serious, morally serious, and a brother in the long search for truth.

What does the framework require?

**Component One:** The believer holds the apostolic deposit firmly. Where Mike's framework diverges at structural points — on the lordship of Christ as the historic creeds confess Him, on the Trinity, on the closed apostolic deposit — the believer does not soften the conviction. The convictions are not on the table for renegotiation, not even with Mike whose intellectual seriousness commands the believer's respect.

**Component Two:** Mike is not his framework. Mike is a brother, a fellow-traveler in the search for truth, a serious thinker whose intelligence and care across decades have produced something worth engaging. The framework, where it diverges from the apostolic deposit, is hated as falsehood — not because Mike is malicious, but because the framework, where it gets the deepest questions wrong, does real harm to Mike at the level of his own ultimate good. Mike is loved. The framework's structural divergences are hated. Both at full intensity.

**Component Three:** Listening, with extra weight. Mike has earned the believer's careful attention over fifty years of work. The believer who treats Mike's framework as if it could be dismissed casually has not understood what Mike has been doing for half a century. The careful listening reveals what is true in the framework (and there is real truth in it; Mike has not been wasting his time), what is missing, what is mis-channeled, what actively contradicts the apostolic deposit. The four diagnostic possibilities apply across the engagement. The believer affirms what is true, introduces what is missing where openings appear, redirects mis-channeled energy where the misdirection can be named without violating the friendship, and resists the structural contradictions where the contradictions can be named honestly.

**Component Four:** The certainties are the apostolic deposit. The prudential is the timing and texture of the engagement with Mike. The believer does not have to deliver the whole gospel in any single conversation. The engagement is over decades, not months. The Spirit's timing is not the believer's timing. What the believer can do is remain in the conversation, remain faithful to the apostolic deposit, remain available, and let the engagement continue across whatever time God allows.

A specific posture for engagement with Mike: the believer can honor what Mike has been building while also being clear about where the building diverges from the foundation Christ laid. The two postures coexist. Mike's mandala system contains real insights that the believer can affirm; the system as a whole, where it positions itself as a complete framework adequate to ultimate questions without the gospel, is not adequate, and the believer can say so plainly without contempt.

## Case 4: The progressive friend who has reached opposite conclusions

Consider a friend, longtime, intelligent, who has reached opposite conclusions on the great moral questions of the moment — who supports legal abortion, who endorses the cultural redefinition of marriage, who has come to see traditional Christianity as substantially complicit in injustice, who has increasingly difficult patience for the believer's continued holding of the apostolic positions.

What does the framework require?

**Component One:** Firm. The believer holds the convictions. He does not negotiate.

**Component Two:** The friend is not his progressive captures. The friend is the person we have known across years, who has real reasons for the path he has taken, whose image-bearer status persists across the disagreement. The friend is loved fiercely. The captures — the ideological framework that has come to position orthodox Christianity as a hate group, the captivity to a culture that has redefined fundamental categories of human existence — are hated as captivities. The progressive flattening (pretending to affirm what we do not affirm to keep the friendship) is rejected. The reactionary flattening (writing the friend off as the enemy) is also rejected.

**Component Three:** Listening with care. The friend has often experienced real wounds — perhaps from the church itself, perhaps from family members who hold conservative positions and held them badly, perhaps from the broader cultural conservatism that has often signaled contempt. The wounds are real. The careful listening allows the believer to honor the wound without affirming the framework that has grown out of it. The friend's concern for the marginalized is real; the redirection is to ask whether the specific frameworks the friend has adopted actually serve the marginalized as the friend hopes. Often they do not, and the redirection can name this.

**Component Four:** Most of the friction is at the prudential level. Underneath, the substantive disagreement is at the certainty level (the personhood of the unborn, the created order of marriage, the lordship of Christ) — but most of the actual conversational friction is over specific applications, specific incidents, specific cultural-moment debates. The taxonomy work allows the believer to hold the certainties firmly while extending appropriate charity at the prudential level, which leaves more space for the friendship to continue than either total agreement or total break would.

The texture of this kind of friendship varies. Some progressive friends remain in the relationship across the disagreement; the friendship deepens in some ways even as it acknowledges the disagreement. Some break it off. The believer does not control which it will be. He controls the faithfulness of his own posture.

## Case 5: The conservative friend who has slid into contempt

Consider a different kind of difficulty: a fellow conservative Christian who has slid into the contempt-culture posture, who treats every progressive as a moral defective, who has lost the capacity to distinguish the captured from the captor, who delivers contempt-laden monologues on social media and at the dinner table about the failings of the cultural left.

What does the framework require?

This is harder than it looks, because the friend is on the believer's own substantive side on most questions. The temptation is to mirror the contempt-culture posture (we are both fighting the same enemies, after all) or to perform a tepid both-sides-have-failings posture that does not actually engage what the friend is doing.

**Component One:** Firm. The believer's convictions are not in question.

**Component Two:** The friend is captured — by the contempt-culture posture itself, which is its own captivity. The friend is treating fellow image-bearers (the progressives he holds in contempt) as the enemy, when the enemy is actually the spiritual evil operating through the cultural moment. The friend has confused the captured with the captor. He is fighting the wrong battle in the wrong way. The captivity here is the captivity to contempt; it is real, and it is hated. The friend himself is loved.

**Component Three:** Listening to what the friend is actually saying. Often the friend is responding to real outrages — actual cultural overreach, actual targeting of Christians, actual injustices — and the underlying outrage is appropriate. The redirect: *I see what you are responding to. The outrage is appropriate. The form your response has taken — the contempt for the people captured by the cultural moment — is the wrong form. Let me show you a better form.* This is one of the most fruitful applications of the framework, because the friend is on the substantive side of the truth and only needs a redirection of how the substance is held.

**Component Four:** The taxonomy work is critical here. The friend is often confusing prudential cultural-tactics judgments with certainty-level convictions, and the confusion is producing the contempt. *Yes, the personhood of the unborn child is a certainty; specific tactics for resisting the cultural moment are prudential; the fact that some Christians are pursuing different tactics than yours does not make them theological compromisers.* The naming of the categories often loosens the contempt-culture grip when the friend is willing to think it through.

This case is one of the most important applications of the framework, because the friend is *us* in another instance. The contempt-culture posture is the corruption that conservative Christianity most needs to repent of in the present moment, and the work of helping fellow conservatives out of it is part of the witness Component Two requires.

## Case 6: The young believer being pressured to soften

Consider a young believer — early twenties, recently graduated, navigating a peer environment in which orthodox Christian convictions are treated as morally beyond the pale. The pressure on the young believer is enormous: to soften the convictions, to perform agnosticism on the great moral questions, to apologize for the tradition that formed him, to absorb the surrounding culture's framings as his own.

What does the framework require? In this case, the believer in the role of mentor or older friend.

**Component One:** Firmness, and the encouragement to firmness. The young believer needs to know that the convictions are firm, that the apostolic deposit teaches what it teaches, that the historic confession is the historic confession, and that his own soft-pedaling is not virtue but capitulation. He needs to see firmness lived in the lives of older believers who have not capitulated, and he needs to hear it named clearly.

**Component Two:** The young believer is loved. His struggle is real; the cultural pressure he faces is real; the cost of holding firm in his peer environment is real. The believer in the older role does not despise the young believer for feeling the pressure; he honors the difficulty while encouraging the firmness.

**Component Three:** Listening to what the young believer is actually struggling with. Sometimes the apparent softening is itself a redirection of real concern — the young believer has noticed something true about how earlier generations of Christians held the convictions badly, and his softening is a reaction against the badness rather than against the convictions themselves. The careful listener can affirm what the young believer has noticed (the badness was real) and redirect the response (the answer to bad firmness is good firmness, not soft capitulation).

**Component Four:** Helping the young believer build the taxonomy. Often the young believer has been pressured to soften everything because he has not yet learned to distinguish the certainties from the prudential. Once he learns the taxonomy, he can hold firm where firmness belongs without holding rigid where rigidity does not belong, and the cultural pressure becomes more manageable.

## Case 7: The older believer hardened into rigidity

The mirror case to Case 6: an older believer who has, over years of cultural conflict, hardened into rigidity. His convictions are firm in a way that has become brittle. He treats every disagreement as a referendum on fidelity. He has lost relationships with fellow Christians over prudential disagreements. He is increasingly isolated within his own church, his own family, his own circle.

What does the framework require?

This case requires the framework's Component Four to be applied with care. The hardened believer often experiences any suggestion that he might be conflating prudential judgments with certainties as itself an attack on the certainties. He has flattened the categories, and the flattening has become identity.

**Component Two:** The hardened believer is loved. His seriousness about the truth is real and right; his rigidity is the captivity that has grown out of the seriousness. The believer in the role of fellow Christian does not despise the hardened brother; he grieves over the rigidity that has limited the brother's witness and frayed the brother's relationships.

**Component Three:** Listening with patience. The hardened believer has often experienced real wounds that have produced the hardening — broken trust within Christian communities, painful personal failures of fellow Christians who softened on substantive matters, the experience of being abandoned by people he thought were on his side. The wounds are real. The careful listening allows the believer to honor the wound without endorsing the hardening that has grown from it.

**Component Four:** Naming the categories. *Brother, I share your conviction on this. But the question of which specific tactic best serves the conviction is not itself a certainty; reasonable Christians may differ; treating the difference as evidence of theological compromise is the rigidity that is costing you relationships you do not need to lose.* The naming has to be done with love, in a relational context where the hardened believer trusts the believer enough to receive it. It is hard work. Sometimes it succeeds. Sometimes it does not.

## Case 8: The cultural moment at large

The fellowship is witnessing in a particular cultural moment — America in 2026, where spiritual warfare is operating openly through institutional channels. The redefinition of fundamental categories of human existence, the ideological capture of educational and corporate and governmental structures, the systematic production of contempt as the default mode of public discourse, the active resistance to the gospel in domains where the gospel was once welcomed.

What does the framework require of believers in this moment?

**Component One:** Hold the convictions firmly. The cultural moment will pressure the believer to soften, in ten different ways, every day. He refuses. The convictions are the convictions. They are not negotiable for any cultural benefit.

**Component Two:** Hate what is to be hated and love what is to be loved. The spiritual evil at work in the cultural moment — the principalities and powers operating through institutional channels, the seducing spirits, the doctrines of devils — is hated. The millions of Americans captured by the captivity, including those whose public presence is most directly causing the cultural decay, are loved. The contempt-culture mirror response that the moment generates on the conservative side is rejected. The believer does not mirror the contempt; he resists the actual enemy.

**Component Three:** Listening, even at scale. The cultural moment is full of voices. Most of them are not worth listening to in any sustained way; the believer's attention is finite, and he is wise to limit his exposure to the channels that are designed to inflame him. But where actual conversations are possible — with neighbors, family, colleagues, friends — the believer listens carefully and responds to what is actually there rather than to the caricature the broader cultural moment is presenting to him.

**Component Four:** The taxonomy holds. The substantive certainties at stake (the personhood of the unborn, the created order of marriage, the lordship of Christ) are held firmly. The prudential judgments about how to engage the cultural moment specifically are held with appropriate humility, and the believer does not break fellowship with fellow Christians who have reached different prudential conclusions about engagement, withdrawal, parallel-institution-building, or political tactics.

## What the witness is for

The witness is not for the believer's vindication. It is not for the moral satisfaction of being on the right side. It is not for the production of measurable outcomes the believer can point to as evidence of his faithfulness.

The witness is for the kingdom Christ is building. *Upon this rock I will build my church; and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it* (Matthew 16:18). The believer is one stone in a building that began at Pentecost and has been under construction in every generation since. His witness — the firm conviction, the directed hatred and love, the careful listening, the taxonomy held straight — is part of the building. Other stones are being laid in places he cannot see, by hands he does not know, in conversations he is not part of. The building is bigger than him. The construction continues whether or not he sees it. His job is to be faithful in the place he has been put, with the people he has been given, in the conversations the Spirit has set in front of him.

This is freeing. The believer does not have to control outcomes. He does not have to convince Leonard, or persuade Mike, or rescue every progressive friend, or repair every conservative who has slid into contempt. He has to be faithful. Faithful in the firmness, faithful in the love-and-hatred direction, faithful in the listening, faithful in the taxonomy. The Spirit does the work the believer cannot do. Time does the work that the moment cannot do. The witness, faithfully maintained over years and decades, accumulates in ways the believer cannot trace and cannot measure.

What the witness leaves to the Spirit is the conversion. What the witness leaves to time is the slow ripening that conversation across years can produce. What the witness keeps for the believer is the discipline of his own posture in the moment.

This is the Christos Civitas Code in practice. It is the relational ethic of conscious participation in the church Christ is building. It is what the apostolic deposit teaches, what the great cloud of witnesses has lived, what is being asked of us now in the cultural moment we have been given. It is harder than either contempt or capitulation. It is also more fruitful, more truthful, more honoring of the God we serve, and more loving of the image-bearers He has placed in our lives.

The work continues. The conversations continue. The witness continues. *Wherefore, my beloved brethren, be ye stedfast, unmoveable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, forasmuch as ye know that your labour is not in vain in the Lord* (1 Corinthians 15:58).

To the fellowship: this is what we are being formed for. This is the work to which we are being called. The book has done what books can do; the rest happens at kitchen tables, in long phone calls, across years, in the actual conversations the Spirit has set before us. We hold the convictions. We hate the captivities. We love the captured. We listen carefully. We keep the categories straight. We are faithful where we have been placed.

*One heart to make Christ King.*
