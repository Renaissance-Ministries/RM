## Purpose

This directory preserves **seed ideas** from Thomas Lee Abshier and other named contributors to Renaissance Ministries, in chronological order, for use as source material by those who elaborate the ideas in the present and those who come after.

Thomas generates a large volume of text across a wide range of topics — theological, political, scientific, philosophical, cultural, constitutional, economic. Many of these texts are thinking-out-loud, voice memos transcribed, fellowship reflections, or short bursts of intuition. They are not finished essays. They are the raw material from which finished essays, position papers, newsletters, Voting Network analyses, and Wisdom Database entries are built.

This archive exists so that the seed does not get lost between the thinking and the finished product. It is a permanent, dated, attributed record of the vision as it was spoken, before it was edited for publication.

The ultimate aim of the vision being preserved here is the actual manifestation of a **Kingdom-of-Heaven culture** on earth — a utopia in the original Greek sense (*ou-topos*, "good place / no place"): a place that does not yet exist, but which the work of this ministry is aimed at bringing into being.

## Relationship to the Wisdom Database

The Founders Vision archive is **not** the Wisdom Database. It feeds it.

| Founders Vision Archive (this folder)       | Wisdom Database (`templates/wisdom_database_operating_system.md`) |
|---------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Chronological by date                       | Organized by domain (theology, politics, science, etc.)          |
| Preserves raw seed language                 | Preserves curated, refined position statements                   |
| One author / contributor per entry          | Synthesized from multiple seed entries and fellowship discussion |
| Permanent; entries are not revised          | Versioned; positions can be refined or reversed                  |
| Private voice; thinking-out-loud permitted  | Public voice; ministry-authoritative                             |
| Raw material                                 | Finished reference                                               |

The workflow is: **seed idea → archived here → curated into Wisdom Database → deployed into public-facing content** (newsletters, CVN analyses, sub-project papers, Christos AI training corpus).

Nothing in this folder is authoritative on its own. Nothing here should be cited as a ministry position. When a seed has been refined into a position, that position lives in the Wisdom Database, and the Wisdom Database entry should cite the seed archive entries it grew from.

## Filename convention

`YYMMDD_topic_slug.md`

- **YYMMDD** — six-digit date the seed was captured. This matches the existing convention used in `CNL_christos_newsletter/articles/`.
- **topic_slug** — short, lowercase, underscore-separated. Aim for 2–5 words. Describe the topic, not the mood. Good: `kingdom_of_heaven_culture`. Bad: `thoughts_today`.
- **.md** — markdown.

Multiple entries on the same day are fine; disambiguate with a trailing suffix only if the topics are genuinely distinct: `260419_topic_a.md`, `260419_topic_b.md`.

Do not put version suffixes in filenames. Entries are not revised — see "Immutability" below.

## Entry structure

Each entry file should contain:

1. **Title** — plain descriptive H1.
2. **Metadata block** — date captured, contributor name, source (spoken / written / fellowship meeting / etc.), and a one-line topic summary.
3. **Seed** — the contributor's actual words, minimally edited. Preserve voice. Do not sand down informality. If the seed is a transcribed voice memo, note that.
4. **Editor's note (optional)** — brief context added by the transcriber or archivist. Clearly flagged as editorial, never mixed with the seed.
5. **Elaboration hooks (optional)** — bullet-list of open questions, adjacent topics, or intended follow-ups that the contributor or a future elaborator should explore. These are invitations, not commitments.

Elaborated material — full essays built from the seed — does **not** belong in this folder. It goes into the appropriate sub-project (CCR, CEA, CHR, CHS, CNL, CVN) or into the Wisdom Database. The entry in this folder can link forward to elaborations once they exist.

## Voice and attribution

- **His words are his words.** When the seed is from Thomas, his phrasing is preserved. Grammar and typos may be lightly corrected for readability; substantive edits are not made.
- **My words are marked as mine.** When an AI (Claude, or others) contributes elaboration, it is clearly flagged — either as an editor's note or in a separate elaboration file that cites this seed.
- **Other contributors are named.** Margo, Isak, Michael Sherman, Susan, Charlie, Leonard, Armond, Todd, and other fellowship members or collaborators who contribute seeds have their entries filed under their own name in the metadata block. Over time, contributor-specific subfolders may be introduced if volume warrants it; for now, all contributors share the flat chronological structure.
- **No anonymous entries.** Every seed has a named human or named AI author. This is a named corpus, not a collective one.

---

## Immutability

Entries are written once and not revised. They are a record of what was said on a given date.

If a contributor's thinking on a topic evolves, the new thinking is filed as a new entry on the date it occurs. The reader can then see the progression. Do not go back and "update" old entries to reflect new understanding — that destroys the record.

Typo fixes are acceptable. Substantive changes are not. When in doubt, write a new entry.

## Hosting and publication

This folder will be published at **www.renaissance-ministries.com** (or a dedicated subdomain) by Isak. The archive is intended to be public. Contributors should write with the awareness that their words are a permanent, searchable, public record.

If a contributor wishes to capture private thinking that should not be public, it does not belong here. Private material stays in personal notes and is not committed to this repository.

## Maintenance

New entries are added by Thomas (and other contributors) as seeds arise. There is no target cadence — the archive grows at the rate the vision is spoken.

Claude (or other AI tools) may be asked to:
- Format a transcribed seed into the entry structure above
- Produce elaboration files that cite one or more seeds and live in the appropriate sub-project folder
- Build indexes (by topic, contributor, or date) as the archive grows large enough to warrant them — initially none are needed

An index file (`INDEX.md`) may be added to this folder once entry count passes ~30; until then, the directory listing and a simple search are sufficient.
