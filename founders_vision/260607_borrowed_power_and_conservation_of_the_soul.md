# All Power Is Borrowed: Derivative Existence and the Conservation of the Soul

**Date:** June 7, 2026
**Source:** Reply-to-Peter fellowship essay (260607, *The Creator Takes a Test Drive*); John 19:11 set beside John 5:19; John 5:26; Acts 17:28
**Status:** Working position — the positive twin of "evil as derivative negation," generalized to all created being

---

## The Central Insight

The framework has long held that evil is *derivative*: it has no independent existence, no power plant of its own, only the borrowed and parasitic use of capacities good in their origin. Peter's letter on the John 5:19 essay forced the positive twin of that principle into the open. It is not only evil that borrows. **Everything that is not God borrows.** All created being — every creature, good or evil, every watt of power anyone in the cosmos has ever spent — is granted and sustained from outside itself, moment by moment, by God.

The two anchoring texts sit side by side. To Pilate, at the height of his apparent power, Jesus says, *Thou couldest have no power at all against me, except it were given thee from above* (John 19:11). And of himself the eternal Son says, *The Son can do nothing of himself* (John 5:19). The pattern runs top to bottom: the Son acts only in perfect correspondence to the Father; the creature acts only on borrowed power. Pilate cannot, the principalities cannot, death itself cannot stand outside the sustaining patience of God.

## The Distinction That Keeps It Orthodox

Derivative does not mean equal in kind. There are two radically different ways of "doing nothing of oneself":

- **The Son's** dependence is the eternal communion of one divine being — the mutual indwelling (*perichoresis*) of Father and Son who are one in being. *As the Father hath life in himself, so hath he given to the Son to have life in himself* (John 5:26).
- **The creature's** dependence is radical contingency — the dependence of a thing that did not have to exist at all and does not for one instant hold itself in being.

The dividing line is **aseity**: *life in himself*, self-existence, life that depends on nothing. Only God has it. The Son has it by eternal gift. The creature never has it; the creature has life *on loan* (Acts 17:28, *in him we live, and move, and have our being*).

## The Conservation of the Soul

The intuition that the individuated person is indestructible — that consciousness, like matter-energy, is conserved and cannot be annihilated — is true, but it is true *derivatively*. The soul persists not because it is a small uncreated god immune to un-making, but because God ordains and sustains its persistence. Conservation, in physics and in the soul alike, is the regularity of God's faithfulness, not a chain on His wrists. The clause that keeps the whole picture orthodox is the one Peter supplied himself: *of course YHWH could end it all at any time.* The conservation law is a standing decision of the Lawgiver, not a constraint upon Him.

## The CPP Connection

This is the same shape the Conscious Point Physics framework gives at the foundation of physics. The Conscious Points are not eternal sovereigns. They persist because the divine consciousness sustains them, Moment by Moment, in their Perceive-Compute-Displace cycle. The believer who borrows all power gratefully from God, speaks in agreement with His Word, and refuses the lie that the soul is its own little god is living *with the grain* of how reality is actually built — from the smallest Conscious Point up to the eternal Son. Derivative existence is one single shape running the whole way up.

## Implication for Christos Civitas

1. **It disciplines power.** Every form of authority — civil, institutional, spiritual, personal — is held on loan and answerable to the One who lent it. This is the theological root of the fellowship's posture toward earthly power: neither worship of it nor terror before it, because no power is anything but borrowed.

2. **It disciplines the tongue.** The spoken word of the believer has real power (Proverbs 18:21; James 3), but *derivative* power — power borrowed by agreement with the Word who already spoke, not generated by the larynx. The name-it-and-claim-it counterfeit is the Pilate error turned inward: forgetting that the power was given from above. Prayer-as-decree is covenantal (a declaration of allegiance), not magical.

3. **It locates the believer.** To know that one's very existence is on loan, sustained every instant, is the end of both pride and despair. The creature owes everything and owns nothing of itself — and is, for exactly that reason, held.
