# Round-One Development Notes

**Purpose.** Round one of the recursive edit on the doctrine spine (2026-07-06) produced four substantive theological contributions from Thomas in response to FLAG-12 and FLAG-14 through FLAG-17. Per the verbatim-preservation discipline, his words are recorded here unedited; the spine (v0.2) integrates them as S10a, S37a, S33a, S39a, S40a, and S40b, and the flag register records the resolutions. These notes exist so that Stage 2 narration drafting can draw on the original phrasing rather than only the compressed spine form.

---

## §1. FLAG-12 — The companion video (Thomas, verbatim)

> "I think your idea of making a companion video with the CPP-type physics metaphor to justify the subtractive/interference energy of the zeroing sin by cancellation and thus restoring perfection and order would be good. That separates the project, philosophy, theory, and theology into another realm."

**Disposition:** Resolved as companion project. The interference-cancellation concept — destructive interference nulling a disordered waveform as the physical picture of order-restoring wrath — is the companion's seed and should anchor its visual language.

## §2. FLAG-14 — The Holy Spirit (Thomas, verbatim)

> "Re: holy Spirit, I see the Holy Spirit as the representative of Christ and the Father. Who Christ sent when He returned to the Father."

**Disposition:** Johannine sending framework (John 14:16–18, 26; 16:7) integrated as S10a; the sanctification work (the gap that occasioned the flag) completed by S37a; personhood guard (teaches, speaks, can be grieved — Ephesians 4:30) retained against the impersonal-force misreading. Open architecture question carried forward: the Spirit's location in the Father→Son→creation layer model is stated functionally in S10a (presence extended into the innermost layer); a fuller ontological statement may emerge in later rounds.

## §3. FLAG-15 — The bodily resurrection and the economy of creation (Thomas, verbatim)

> "The bodily resurrection is an important part of 'the end of the story.' It is a very clear promise/prophecy/plan. It makes the entire arc of reality make sense by putting so much energy into creating a creation that was perfect and joyful from the beginning, but was ruined because of man's imperfect/immature character. The whole story makes sense if it was meant to be an eternal relationship. I think the physical universe is the most complex and articulated form of the universe, and returning to it to populate and live in the joy of the Lord in it makes sense in terms of effort and economy. It is an organism that is growing and is in the maturation/adolescent phase rather than the adult phase."

**Disposition:** Integrated as S40a (the resurrection) and S40b (the economy-of-effort argument). Textual anchor supplied in review: Romans 8:19–23 — the creation groaning and travailing toward the manifestation of the sons of God — which gives the adolescent-organism framing a direct Pauline basis. Tradition note: this is the anti-gnostic argument (redemption of creation, not from it; Irenaeus) in Thomas's vocabulary. Production note: this repairs the series' closing image — the door opens onto a world, not onto light.

## §4. FLAG-16 — The historical anchor (Thomas, verbatim)

> "The one anchor I put in regarding the historic Jesus was the caveat that the wrath must be directed at a flesh man to be resonant and effective in zeroing the sin. The reference to the historic man, Jesus, God incarnate, as that man, that substitute offering, is the place where God connects with humanity and history."

**Disposition:** The resonance requirement covers the structural necessity (S23–S25); S33a adds the evidentiary anchor (crucified under Pontius Pilate; seen by above five hundred, 1 Corinthians 15:3–8) to close the "beautiful myth" escape hatch. Both retained: the necessity and the receipt.

## §5. FLAG-17 — Love of neighbor and the structure of the commandments (Thomas, verbatim)

> "Of course, love of neighbor as the flesh surrogate of God, the human face put on loving God. And the experience of loving man, treating him/her well, is experienced by God. So loving neighbor and giving him/her the experience of being loved is what God feels. Thus, the first commandment, to love the lord your God with heart, mind, and strength, is actually the only law. Loving one's neighbor as oneself is redundant since the other and oneself are also parts of God that He experiences. If we put the distinctions back on, we see that we are to love God, neighbor, and self, and we have to think about what to do in each relationship, and that is good, but for understanding the structure of the creation, it is good to take down the illusion and look at the underlying structure of creation for a moment, and then go back to living it out, loving neighbor as self, and loving God, for His law, His beauty, justice, plan, creation, and His love."

**Disposition:** Integrated as S39a with Matthew 25:40 as the proof-text ("Inasmuch as ye have done it unto one of the least of these my brethren, ye have done it unto me" — the structural claim in Christ's own voice). The "human face" phrase retained in the spine wording. Guard registered: the distinctions-restored move is load-bearing; Stage 2 narration must never state the structural reduction (first commandment as the only law) without the restoration (live it out as two). Tradition note: Augustine's ordo amoris (loving the neighbor in God and for God) is the historic form of the insight; Thomas's reduction is stronger but compatible.
