## 1. The decision and the reason

The Renaissance Ministries content corpus is licensed under **Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International (CC BY-SA 4.0)**. The full legal text is in `/LICENSE` and is also available at <https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/legalcode>.

This is a deliberate choice, not a default. Three options were considered: CC BY 4.0 (most permissive), CC BY-SA 4.0 (attribution + share-alike, no commercial restriction), and CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 (attribution + share-alike + non-commercial). CC BY-SA 4.0 was selected because it best operationalizes the Great Commission instinct that drives this entire project:

> Freely ye have received, freely give. (Matthew 10:8)

Concretely, CC BY-SA 4.0:

- **Permits anyone, anywhere, to use this material** — copy it, redistribute it, remix it, build upon it, translate it, adapt it for new contexts, and yes, train artificial intelligences on it. No request need be made; no payment is owed.
- **Permits commercial use.** A publisher who wants to include fellowship essays in a book may do so. A school that wants to charge tuition for a course built on this material may do so. The Kingdom is not advanced by paywalls.
- **Requires attribution.** The work must be credited to its author. Readers must be able to trace any derivative back to its source. (See §4 below for how to attribute.)
- **Requires that derivatives stay open.** Anyone who redistributes the material — modified or unmodified — must license the result under CC BY-SA 4.0 or a license compatible with it. The work cannot be absorbed into a closed proprietary system. What was given freely must be passed on freely.

This last requirement is the share-alike clause. It is the difference between giving a gift and selling an asset. We give the gift; the share-alike clause ensures the gift remains a gift downstream.

## 3. What is licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0 — and what is not

The CC BY-SA 4.0 license applies to **original Renaissance Ministries content authored by Thomas Lee Abshier and any co-authors formally credited in a given file**. This includes:

- Fellowship essays under `Fellowship_essays/` and across the `Christos_*` sub-projects.
- Newsletter articles under `CNL_christos_newsletter/articles/`.
- Founding vision material under `founders_vision/`.
- Templates, operating system documents, and the Theological Grammar under `templates/`.
- Sub-project operating systems and content under `CCR_*`, `CEA_*`, `CHR_*`, `CHS_*`, `CNL_*`, `CVN_*`.
- Repository-level documents (`README.md`, `RM_bootup.md`, `MODULES.md`, `RM_Content_Catalog.md`, `CLAUDE.md`).
- Code in the `client/` and `server/` directories that supports the Christos Voting Network application, except where a different license is declared in a sub-directory.

The license **does not extend** to the following categories. Each is described below.

### 3.1 Fellowship-member contributions

Direct quotations from fellowship members — Susan, Charlie Gutierrez, Isak, Leonard, Armond Boulware, Michael Sherman, Margo, and others — are reproduced in fellowship-discussion essays with their consent in the context of fellowship participation. Their words remain their own. Each speaker retains copyright in their own utterances. The CC BY-SA 4.0 license **does not extend** to those quoted contributions; downstream users who wish to reproduce or remix a fellowship-member contribution beyond fair-use commentary should seek the speaker's permission directly. Renaissance Ministries credits each speaker by name when their words are reproduced and will assist in connecting interested parties to the speaker.

### 3.2 AI collaboration

Substantial portions of this corpus are produced through human-AI collaboration. The AI collaboration model is documented in `RM_bootup.md` §9. Under United States copyright law, AI-generated text as such is not eligible for copyright; the human-authored elements — direction, selection, editing, theological framing, and integration — are. Content drafted with AI assistance under Thomas's editorial direction is treated as Thomas's original authored work for the purpose of this license. The AI collaboration is acknowledged but does not generate independent copyright and does not change the licensing of the resulting work.

### 3.3 Third-party material quoted under fair use

The corpus engages many copyrighted external sources — lectures, interviews, podcasts, books, articles, videos. Those sources are quoted, summarized, and discussed under the fair-use commentary doctrine, governed by `templates/copyright_discipline.md`. The CC BY-SA 4.0 license **does not and cannot extend** to third-party material that is not Renaissance Ministries' to license. A reader who wishes to reuse a quoted passage from, for example, a Denver Snuffer lecture or a PragerU interview must respect the original copyright holder's rights and obtain permission directly from them. Our license governs only our commentary, summary, and original analysis.

### 3.4 Scripture

All King James Version scripture quoted in this corpus is in the public domain in the United States and most jurisdictions. It is freely usable by anyone for any purpose. Where modern translations are quoted, they appear under fair-use terms and remain the property of their respective copyright holders.

### 3.5 The CPP repository is separately governed

The Conscious Point Physics work — published in a separate repository at <https://github.com/Hyperphysics-Institute/CPP> — is **not** governed by this license file. CPP material may need to comply with the licensing terms of academic journals where papers are submitted; treat the CPP repository as having its own governance and consult its own license declaration. Where this RM repository discusses or references CPP material, the RM-side discussion is licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0; the CPP source material it references is governed by the CPP repository's own terms.

## 4. How to attribute

A reader who copies, redistributes, remixes, or builds upon this material must give attribution. The minimum acceptable attribution is:

> **Title (or description) of the work.** Thomas Lee Abshier, *Renaissance Ministries.* Licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0. <https://www.renaissance-ministries.com> · <https://github.com/Renaissance-Ministries/RM>

For example, a quotation from a fellowship essay might be attributed as:

> Excerpt from "The Kingdom of God and Kingdoms of Men," Thomas Lee Abshier, *Renaissance Ministries.* Licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0. <https://www.renaissance-ministries.com>

Three additional points:

- **Indicate modifications.** If you have adapted, abridged, translated, or otherwise modified the work, say so. CC BY-SA 4.0 §3(a)(1)(B) requires this; we ask you to do it cleanly enough that a reader can tell what is yours and what is ours.
- **Link to the license.** The full license text is at <https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/>. Linking it once is sufficient.
- **Pass the license forward.** A derivative work must itself be licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0 or a license listed by Creative Commons as compatible. (This is the share-alike requirement.)

## 5. How to contribute

Contributions to the Renaissance Ministries corpus — pull requests, edits, new essays, new translations — are welcome. By contributing material to this repository, the contributor agrees that:

1. The contributed material is the contributor's own original work, or is properly attributed and permitted for inclusion;
2. The contributed material is released under the same CC BY-SA 4.0 license that governs the rest of the corpus, with the contributor credited as a co-author of the specific file or section to which they contributed.

If a contributor wishes to retain different terms over their contribution, that contribution should not be merged into the main repository. Instead, the contributor should publish the work in their own repository under their own license and submit a link or excerpt that we can engage under our normal copyright discipline (`templates/copyright_discipline.md`).

## 6. Commercial use, special licensing, and pastoral inquiries

Commercial use of this material is **already permitted** under CC BY-SA 4.0 with attribution and share-alike. No special license is needed for:

- Including essays in a printed book, anthology, or curriculum (commercial or otherwise).
- Training a public-facing AI system on this corpus.
- Quoting, translating, or adapting material for a podcast, video, course, or blog.
- Distributing the corpus through any channel, paid or free.

What CC BY-SA 4.0 does **not** permit is closed proprietary derivation — building a closed product that incorporates this material without itself being released under CC BY-SA 4.0. If a party has a use case that genuinely requires a different license (for example, integration with a closed-source system that cannot be released under CC BY-SA 4.0), Renaissance Ministries will consider granting an additional, separately negotiated license. Inquiries of that kind, and any other questions about the corpus, should be directed to:
